## Biography
Gareth Hinds (born 1971) is an American illustrator and graphic novelist best known for his vivid adaptations of literary classics into comics form. Raised in small-town Vermont, he was a quiet, art-obsessed child who spent countless hours drawing and making homemade comics. He went on to study illustration at Rochester Institute of Technology and Parsons School of Design, where he refined the draftsmanship and storytelling skills that would later define his work. After college, Hinds spent more than a decade as an artist in the video game industry, creating characters, animations, and environments for PC and console titles before turning full-time to books.

Hinds built his reputation through ambitious graphic novel retellings of canonical texts such as “Beowulf,” “The Odyssey,” “The Iliad,” “Romeo and Juliet,” “Macbeth,” and “Poe: Stories and Poems.” His books are widely used in English classrooms for the way they balance fidelity to the originals with visual dynamism, helping new readers enter demanding works through a strongly narrative, cinematic style. His illustrations have been exhibited by institutions including the Society of Illustrators and the New York Historical Society, and he has received honors such as the Boston Public Library’s Literary Lights for Children award.

Throughout his career, Hinds has emphasized emotional clarity, historical atmosphere, and respect for the source material, often varying his visual style from project to project to suit each text’s tone and setting. He also visits schools and festivals to speak about adaptation, visual storytelling, and the creative process, positioning himself as both a practicing artist and an advocate for comics as serious literature.
