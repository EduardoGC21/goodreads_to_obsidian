## Biography
David Hernández de la Fuente (born 1974 in Madrid) is a Spanish writer, translator, and scholar of the ancient world whose career bridges academic research and literary creation. Trained at the Complutense University of Madrid, he earned degrees in Classical Philology, Hispanic Philology, and Law, followed by doctorates in Classical Philology and in Social History/ Sociology of Antiquity. He has taught at institutions such as Universidad Carlos III de Madrid, the University of Potsdam, UNED, Saint Louis University’s Madrid campus, and is currently Professor of Greek Philology in the Department of Classics at the Complutense University of Madrid, where his work centers on Greek religion and mythology, Late Antique literature and society, and the history of Platonism.([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Hern%C3%A1ndez_de_la_Fuente?utm_source=openai))

Internationally active, Hernández de la Fuente has been a visiting scholar or lecturer at universities and research centers across Europe and the United States, including Columbia University, the University of Florence, CNRS in Paris, and the Free University of Berlin. His research and teaching have been recognized with numerous awards and competitive fellowships, such as the Prize of the Pastor Foundation for Classical Studies and the Burgen Scholarship Award of the Academia Europaea, as well as Spanish national research grants like the Juan de la Cierva, Alexander von Humboldt, and Ramón y Cajal programs.([steiner-verlag.de](https://www.steiner-verlag.de/en/person/David-Hernandez-de-la-Fuente-1391078?utm_source=openai))

Alongside his academic production—spanning many books and dozens of articles on Greek religion, oracles, Neoplatonism, and Late Antiquity—he is known to a wider readership through essays and literary works that bring the classical world to contemporary audiences. Books such as "Oráculos griegos" exemplify his effort to interpret ancient texts and religious practices for modern readers, while his fiction and critical essays contribute to debates on classical tradition in modern culture. He also serves on editorial boards and directs scholarly series and journals, helping shape current research on Byzantium, Late Antiquity, and classical reception.([instituthumanitats.org](https://www.instituthumanitats.org/es/ponentes/david-hernandez-de-la-fuente?utm_source=openai))
