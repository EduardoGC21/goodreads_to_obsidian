## Biography
Alberto Bernabé Pajares (born 1946) is a Spanish classical philologist and historian of ancient religion whose work has been central to the modern study of Greek Orphism, archaic Greek epic, and Indo‑European linguistics. Born in Huelva in 1946, he developed his academic career at the Complutense University of Madrid, where he became Professor (later Emeritus Professor) of Greek Philology and directed the Department of Greek Philology and Indo‑European Linguistics. Over several decades he has led research projects on Greek religion, especially the figure of Orpheus and the cult of Dionysus, while also contributing to Mycenaean, Hittite and broader Indo‑European studies.

Bernabé has played a prominent institutional role in Spanish linguistics and classical studies. He served as president of the Spanish Society of Linguistics, helped edit the major Greek–Spanish Dictionary of the CSIC, and has long been associated with the journal Revista Española de Lingüística. As president of the Centro de Estudios del Próximo Oriente and a frequent lecturer in Europe and the Americas, he has fostered dialogue between classical philology, religious history, and comparative linguistics.

A prolific author, Bernabé has produced critical editions of early Greek epic fragments, key corpora of Orphic testimonies, and influential monographs and translations that have shaped contemporary understanding of Greek literature and religion. Works such as his editions of archaic epic fragments, his studies on Orphic texts, and his translations and commentaries on Greek and Hittite sources—including material on the Hittite myths treated in "Mitos hititas: Entre Oriente y Occidente"—have made him a major reference for scholars interested in how ancient Mediterranean and Near Eastern mythologies intersect and how these traditions illuminate the intellectual and religious landscape of the ancient world.
