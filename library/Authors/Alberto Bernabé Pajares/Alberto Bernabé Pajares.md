## Biography
Alberto Bernabé Pajares (1946– ) is a Spanish classical philologist and historian of ancient religions whose work has been central to the modern study of Orphism, archaic Greek epic, and Indo‑European linguistics. Born in Huelva, he pursued a career in Greek philology that led him to the Complutense University of Madrid, where he became professor of Greek language and literature and later directed the Department of Greek Philology and Indo‑European Linguistics. Over decades of teaching and supervision he helped shape several generations of Spanish classicists and scholars of the ancient Mediterranean.

Bernabé has held a number of prominent scholarly roles, including the presidency of the Spanish Society of Linguistics and leadership of the Center for Near Eastern Studies, and he has contributed to large collective projects such as the Diccionario Griego‑Español and the editorial board of the Revista Española de Lingüística. Internationally he is known for his critical editions of early Greek epic and Orphic texts, his manuals on textual criticism and Indo‑European linguistics, and his translations and commentaries on Greek, Hittite, and other ancient texts. Works like his studies of the Orphic gold tablets and his later synthesis on Hittite myths, including “Mitos hititas: Entre Oriente y Occidente,” situate Greek and Anatolian traditions within a wider Near Eastern context and have made complex specialist material accessible to students and non‑specialists.

Beyond his publications, Bernabé’s significance lies in the way he bridges philology, religious studies, and comparative Indo‑European scholarship. His collaborations with colleagues in Spain and abroad, and his sustained involvement in academic societies and editorial projects, have reinforced connections between classical studies, oriental studies, and linguistics, making him a key intermediary between Spanish‑language scholarship and the broader international research community.
