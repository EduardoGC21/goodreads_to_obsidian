## Biography
Inio Asano (born 1980) is a Japanese manga artist known for his psychologically rich, emotionally raw depictions of everyday life, youth, and alienation. Emerging in the early 2000s, he quickly gained attention for blending delicate, almost photographic artwork with stories that probe anxiety, depression, and the difficulty of human connection in modern society. His work often features ordinary characters facing subtle but crushing pressures from family, school, and work, rendered with a mix of dark humor and deep empathy.

Asano’s significance lies in how he pushes the boundaries of what commercial manga can address, treating themes such as mental illness, abuse, and disillusionment with unusual frankness. "Goodnight Punpun" in particular became a landmark series for its experimental visual symbolism—such as depicting the protagonist as a simple bird-like figure—and its unflinching portrayal of a boy’s troubled path into adulthood. Through such works, Asano has influenced both readers and younger artists, standing out as a major voice in contemporary Japanese comics whose stories capture the emotional texture of life in post-bubble, urban Japan.
