## Biography
Françoise Bonardel (born 1947 in Joigny, France) is a French philosopher of religion whose work bridges academic rigor and spiritual traditions often considered marginal to mainstream philosophy. Agrégée de philosophie and holder of a state doctorate in letters and human sciences, she devoted her early research to the presence of alchemical thought in modern and contemporary culture, work later developed in her influential study *Philosophie de l’alchimie: Grand Œuvre et modernité*. As professor at the University Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne, she taught philosophy of religions from 1990 to 2010, helping to shape a generation of students attentive to symbolism, myth, and esoteric currents in Western culture.

Bonardel is recognized as a major contemporary interpreter of hermetism, alchemy, gnosis, and Buddhism, exploring how these traditions illuminate questions of identity, culture, and spiritual transformation in late modernity. Her books, including *La voie hermétique* and *Philosopher par le feu*, use historical erudition and careful textual analysis to argue that symbolic practices and the idea of “transmutation” can still nourish philosophical reflection today. Closely engaged with the work of C. G. Jung, she examines how depth psychology reactivates ancient gnostic themes, especially in her study *Jung et la gnose*.

Beyond her university career, Bonardel has been active in the broader intellectual and spiritual landscape, notably through her involvement with the Institut d’Études Bouddhiques, where she teaches on the encounter between Buddhism and European thought. Her writings and public dialogues, including conversations with scientists and religious thinkers, seek to reconnect philosophy with wisdom traditions without abandoning critical reason. Works such as *La voie hermétique: Introduction à la philosophie d’Hermès* exemplify her effort to recover a specifically European spiritual heritage while opening it to intercultural and interreligious dialogue.
