## Biography
Carlos García Gual (born 1943 in Palma de Mallorca) is a Spanish writer, philologist, and one of the leading contemporary interpreters of classical Greek culture and mythology. Trained with major Spanish Hellenists, he became a professor of Greek Philology and later emeritus professor at the Complutense University of Madrid, after holding chairs at the universities of Granada, Barcelona, and the UNED. His academic work ranges from ancient philosophy to classical and medieval literature, with a particular focus on myth, the ancient novel, and the heroic tradition, reflected in studies such as his books on Prometheus and on the death and transformation of heroes in Greek literature.

Alongside his scholarly output, García Gual has been highly influential as a translator and cultural mediator. He has translated numerous classical Greek and some medieval texts into Spanish, twice receiving Spain’s National Translation Prize for the quality and breadth of his work. As an editor and advisor to the Biblioteca Clásica Gredos and other collections, he has helped shape how generations of Spanish readers encounter the Greek and Latin classics. He is also a prominent literary critic, publishing essays and reviews in major cultural journals and newspapers, and has been elected a full member of the Real Academia Española.

García Gual’s writings aim to bring the ancient world into dialogue with modern readers, highlighting the lasting power of myths, heroes, and narrative forms. Works such as “La muerte de los héroes” and his studies of Prometheus use specific figures and stories to explore how classical themes have been reinterpreted across time, from antiquity to modern literature. Through his teaching, translations, and essays, he has played a central role in sustaining and renewing interest in the classics within the Spanish-speaking world.
