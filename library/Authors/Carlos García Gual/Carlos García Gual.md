## Biography
Carlos García Gual (1943–) is a Spanish philologist, classical scholar, and prolific essayist best known for his work on ancient Greek myth, literature, and philosophy. Born in Palma de Mallorca, he pursued classical studies and went on to become one of Spain’s leading interpreters of the ancient world, combining rigorous philological training with an accessible, essayistic style that brought Greek and Roman culture to a wide readership.

Over the course of his academic career, García Gual has taught Greek and classical literature at several universities, most prominently at the Complutense University of Madrid, where he helped shape generations of students in classical studies. He is an influential translator into Spanish of key Greek authors, contributing major versions of works by Homer, Hesiod, Apollonius of Rhodes, and other canonical writers. His essays and monographs, including studies such as *La muerte de los héroes* and his work on Prometheus and mythic tradition, explore how ancient narratives about heroes, gods, and philosophers illuminate enduring human concerns about fate, justice, and the search for meaning.

García Gual’s significance extends beyond the university through his roles in cultural institutions, editorial boards, and public-facing essays on the classical heritage in modern culture. His election to the Real Academia Española recognized both his scholarly achievements and his contribution to contemporary Spanish letters, underscoring his status as a key mediator between the classical past and present-day readers.
