## Biography
Johannes Grave (born 1976 in Thuine, Emsland) is a German art historian whose work has reshaped contemporary understanding of European painting from the Early Renaissance to the Romantic era. Educated in art history, medieval Latin, medieval history, and philosophy at the University of Freiburg, he continued his training through a research stay in Rome and early appointments at the Friedrich Schiller University Jena and the University of Basel, where he completed his habilitation. His career has combined rigorous scholarship with a strong engagement in research institutions and cross‑disciplinary projects, particularly around the cultural world of Weimar and Jena around 1800.

Grave has held influential academic positions, including professor of art history at Bielefeld University and later professor of Modern Art History at Friedrich Schiller University Jena. He has been active internationally as deputy director of the German Forum for Art History in Paris and as co‑curator of major exhibitions, notably on Caspar David Friedrich, where his monograph on the artist has become a key reference for understanding Romantic landscape painting and its philosophical, religious, and emotional dimensions. His broader research spans Giovanni Bellini, image theory, and the temporality of visual perception, and he has published widely on these topics.

Beyond his scholarly writing, Grave plays a prominent role in the German and European research landscape. He has served on committees of the Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft (DFG), becoming a member of its Senate and Grants Committee and, starting in 2024, a vice president representing the humanities. He is closely involved with the Klassik Stiftung Weimar, chairing its academic advisory board, and is a founding member of the Centre for Classical Research in Weimar. Recognition of his contributions includes the prestigious Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz Prize (2020) and election to the Academia Europaea, underscoring his standing as one of the leading art historians of his generation.
