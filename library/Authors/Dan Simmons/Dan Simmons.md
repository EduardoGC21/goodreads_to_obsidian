## Biography
Dan Simmons (born 1948) is an American author best known for his genre-spanning work in science fiction, horror, and fantasy. Before turning to writing full time, he worked for many years as a teacher, an experience that sharpened his interest in literature, history, and human psychology—all recurring elements in his fiction. He first gained wide recognition in the 1980s, quickly establishing a reputation for ambitious narratives that blend literary depth with popular storytelling.

Simmons’s novel "Hyperion" and its sequels form the acclaimed Hyperion Cantos, a landmark in modern science fiction that weaves together pilgrimage, politics, religion, and poetry in a far-future setting. His work often pays explicit homage to classic authors and forms, using intertextual references to explore how stories shape cultures and individuals. Beyond science fiction, Simmons has written notable horror and historical novels, demonstrating a consistent interest in moral complexity, the consequences of power, and the resilience of human relationships under extreme pressure.

Widely regarded as a significant figure in late 20th- and early 21st-century speculative fiction, Simmons has received major genre awards and developed a dedicated international readership. His career reflects a persistent effort to bridge the gap between genre and literary fiction, using richly drawn characters and carefully researched settings to situate personal dramas within broader cultural and historical contexts.
