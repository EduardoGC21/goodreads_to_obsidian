## Biography
Dan Simmons (born 1948) is an American author best known for blending genres—science fiction, horror, fantasy, crime, and historical fiction—into richly researched, character‑driven narratives. Raised in the Midwest, he worked for years as a teacher and educational administrator before turning to writing full time, a background that shaped his interest in psychology, moral choice, and the formative power of stories. His early breakthrough came with the horror novel *Song of Kali*, but his reputation was firmly established by works like *Hyperion*, which combined space opera with literary and poetic influences, notably drawing on John Keats and classical myth.

Simmons is recognized for his meticulous attention to historical and scientific detail, often setting his novels against vividly rendered real-world events or plausible futures. He has explored themes such as colonialism, ecological collapse, religious faith, and the ethics of power, while frequently focusing on small groups of individuals caught within vast, often oppressive systems. Over his career he has won major genre awards, including the Hugo, Nebula, Bram Stoker, and World Fantasy Awards, and he is regarded as a significant figure in late 20th- and early 21st‑century speculative fiction.
