## Biography
Alix Paré is an art historian and lecturer specializing in Western painting from the seventeenth through the twentieth centuries. Trained within the French museum world, she has held positions at the Musée du Louvre in Paris and at the Château de Versailles, where she developed a deep familiarity with the visual cultures, iconography, and curatorial practices of early modern and modern European art. Her work bridges scholarly research and public dissemination, presenting complex art historical themes in ways that are accessible to general audiences while remaining grounded in rigorous scholarship.

Paré has become particularly known for her studies of nineteenth‑century imagery and the fantastic, with a focus on artists whose work moves between realism, symbolism, and the supernatural. Her book *The Fantastic Gustave Doré* showcases her ability to reinterpret a canonical illustrator for contemporary readers, situating Doré’s prolific output within broader cultural, literary, and visual traditions. Across her publications, she curates large bodies of images into coherent narratives that illuminate how painting and printmaking have shaped understandings of spirituality, imagination, and the uncanny in European art history.

Beyond individual monographs, Alix Paré’s significance lies in the way she connects museum practice, academic research, and publishing. By selecting, organizing, and interpreting hundreds of works for richly illustrated volumes, she helps preserve and recontextualize visual heritage for a wide readership. Her collaborations with major European institutions and publishers position her as a key mediator between collections, specialists, and lay readers interested in the intersections of art, literature, and esoteric or fantastical themes.
