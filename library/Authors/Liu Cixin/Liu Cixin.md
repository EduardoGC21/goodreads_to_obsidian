## Biography
Liu Cixin (born 1963) is a Chinese science fiction author widely credited with bringing Chinese SF to global prominence. Trained as a power plant engineer, he spent much of his early career working at a power station in Shanxi Province, writing fiction in his spare time. His technical background and experience of China’s rapid industrialization deeply shaped his work, infusing it with hard-science rigor, large-scale engineering imagination, and a sense of historical and civilizational transformation.

Liu’s breakthrough came with the "Remembrance of Earth’s Past" trilogy—beginning with "The Three-Body Problem" and continuing through "The Dark Forest" and "Death’s End"—which combined astrophysics, game theory, and cosmic-scale storytelling to explore humanity’s place in a vast, often indifferent universe. The series earned him major awards, including the Hugo Award for Best Novel, making him one of the first Asian authors to receive that honor and cementing his reputation as a central figure in contemporary science fiction. Though he is known to be private about his personal life, his work has sparked extensive dialogue about science, power, and the future of civilization, influencing writers, scientists, and policymakers both within China and internationally.
