## Biography
Junji Ito (1963–) is a Japanese horror manga artist renowned for his meticulous linework, unsettling imagination, and ability to turn everyday anxieties into cosmic terror. Born in Gifu Prefecture, he grew up reading horror manga and stories, developing an early fascination with the genre while working as a dental technician before debuting professionally. His breakout work "Tomie" in the late 1980s quickly established him as a major new voice in horror, known for blending body horror, psychological unease, and surreal imagery.

Over the following decades, Ito became one of the most influential figures in modern horror comics worldwide. Works like "Uzumaki"—a town’s descent into madness through an obsession with spirals—and his adaptation of Osamu Dazai’s "No Longer Human" showcase his range, from original cosmic grotesquerie to sensitive, disturbing literary reinterpretation. His stories often explore isolation, obsession, and the fragility of human sanity, while his characters’ relationships are pushed to extremes by supernatural or inexplicable forces. Ito’s influence extends beyond manga into film, animation, and international horror culture, earning him multiple awards and a devoted global readership.

Despite the disturbing nature of his work, Ito is frequently described in interviews and public appearances as modest and soft-spoken, contrasting sharply with his terrifying creations. His continued output of short stories, longer serials, and adaptations has cemented his status as a central figure in contemporary horror, inspiring artists, writers, and filmmakers around the world.
