## Biography
Junji Ito (1963– ) is a Japanese horror manga artist born in Gifu Prefecture, Japan. He trained and worked as a dental technician before transitioning to manga full time, developing a meticulous linework style that mirrors the clinical precision of his earlier profession. Inspired by childhood readings of Kazuo Umezu and the cosmic dread of H. P. Lovecraft, Ito began submitting short horror stories to magazines, quickly gaining attention for his ability to turn everyday anxieties and bodily vulnerability into surreal, escalating nightmare scenarios.

Ito’s breakthrough came with serializations like *Tomie* and later *Uzumaki*, the latter cementing his international reputation for fusing psychological horror with visually inventive grotesquerie. His adaptations and reinterpretations of existing literature, such as *No Longer Human*, show his interest in the inner lives of damaged, alienated people as much as in monsters and curses, giving his work a tragic human core. Over the decades he has become one of the most influential figures in modern horror comics, shaping both Japanese and global horror aesthetics, and receiving numerous domestic and international awards that recognize his impact on the medium.

Despite his often disturbing subject matter, accounts from colleagues and interviews present Ito as soft-spoken and even gentle in person, deeply curious about the fears and obsessions that underlie ordinary life. His stories frequently explore small-town communities, intimate relationships, and family ties pushed to extremes by uncanny forces, reflecting both his rural upbringing and his fascination with how social bonds fray under pressure. This combination of personal background, technical craft, and philosophical engagement with fear has made him a singular voice in contemporary horror storytelling.
