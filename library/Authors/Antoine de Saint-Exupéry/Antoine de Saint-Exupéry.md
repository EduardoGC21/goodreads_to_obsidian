## Biography
Antoine de Saint-Exupéry (1900–1944) was a French writer, pioneering aviator, and journalist whose life and work were deeply shaped by the early era of aviation. Born into an aristocratic family in Lyon, he developed a fascination with flying at a young age and later became a commercial pilot, flying mail routes over the Sahara and South America. These dangerous and often solitary flights informed much of his writing, blending technical experience with philosophical reflection.

Saint-Exupéry gained international recognition with works that drew on his aviation career and his humanistic outlook, including "Night Flight" and "Wind, Sand and Stars." His most famous book, "Le Petit Prince" (known in Spanish as "El Principito"), combined poetic simplicity with existential themes, reflecting his concern with friendship, responsibility, and the loss of childhood wonder. During World War II he flew reconnaissance missions for the Free French forces; he disappeared in 1944 during a flight over the Mediterranean, and his presumed death gave a mythic, unfinished quality to his life story. He is remembered both as a brave pilot and as a writer whose slim body of work had a lasting impact on world literature.
