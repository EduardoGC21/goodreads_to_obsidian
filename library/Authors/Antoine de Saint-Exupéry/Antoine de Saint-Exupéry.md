## Biography
Antoine de Saint-Exupéry (1900–1944) was a French writer, pioneering aviator, and journalist whose life and work were deeply shaped by the early decades of powered flight and the upheavals of the two World Wars. Born into an aristocratic family in Lyon, he trained as a pilot in the French Air Force and went on to fly mail routes across Africa and South America, experiences that furnished the material and philosophical background for much of his writing. His dual identity as both pilot and author made him a distinctive voice, blending technical familiarity with flight and a poetic, humanistic reflection on solitude, friendship, duty, and mortality.

As an author, Saint-Exupéry is best known for "Le Petit Prince" ("The Little Prince"), of which "El Principito" is a Spanish edition, a tale that combines fable, allegory, and personal reflection to explore themes of innocence, love, and the loss of childhood wonder. Beyond this celebrated work, his novels and essays—such as "Vol de nuit" ("Night Flight") and "Terre des hommes" ("Wind, Sand and Stars")—examined the moral and emotional dimensions of aviation and human responsibility, earning him international recognition and literary awards. He maintained close ties with fellow pilots and intellectuals, and his relationships, including his marriage to Consuelo de Saint-Exupéry, often informed the emotional texture of his characters and stories.

During World War II, Saint-Exupéry served as a reconnaissance pilot for the French Air Force, later flying missions for the Free French forces despite being over the usual age and having health issues. His commitment to flying in wartime was both a personal vocation and an expression of his belief in service and sacrifice. On July 31, 1944, he disappeared during a reconnaissance mission over the Mediterranean, and his death remained a mystery for decades, reinforcing his mythic status as an aviator-writer who vanished in the skies he had so often described. His legacy endures as that of a humanist thinker and stylist whose stories continue to bridge the worlds of childhood and adulthood, technology and spirituality.
