## Biography
Kayoko Takagi is a Japanese scholar and translator specializing in Japanese language, literature, and traditional culture. Based in Spain, she serves as a professor of Japanese language and culture at the Universidad Autónoma de Madrid and works as a researcher at that university's Center for East Asian Studies, helping to build academic and cultural bridges between Japan and the Spanish‑speaking world. Her academic focus and teaching connect historical Japanese texts, folklore, and contemporary cultural studies for students encountering Japan’s traditions for the first time.

Beyond her university work, Takagi has been active as a translator and editor, making classic Japanese stories and cultural materials accessible to Spanish readers. In "Cuentos tradicionales de Japón," she is responsible for the selection, introduction, notes, and translation from Japanese, curating a corpus of folktales that conveys both the narrative charm of the originals and their broader mythological and aesthetic context. Through this and related work, she has contributed to a deeper appreciation of Japanese folklore, aesthetics, and worldview in the Hispanic world, mediating between scholarly rigor and the needs of a general readership.

Takagi’s career reflects a commitment to cross‑cultural dialogue, particularly between Japan and Spain.
