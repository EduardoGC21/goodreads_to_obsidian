## Biography
Gabriel García-Noblejas Sánchez-Cendal (b. 1966) is a Spanish scholar, translator, and professor whose work has played a key role in presenting classical Chinese culture to Spanish-speaking readers. Born in Oviedo, he received a broad humanistic education, studying with Jesuits in his hometown before completing Hispanic Philology in Oviedo and at Durham University in England, as well as French language and culture studies in Pau, France. In the 1990s he deepened his engagement with China by studying Chinese language and civilization at the Beijing Foreign Studies University, experiences that shaped his lifelong dedication to Chinese literature and thought.

After earning his doctorate in 2002 with a thesis on translating Chinese literature into Spanish, García-Noblejas joined the University of Granada, where he teaches Chinese–Spanish translation and Chinese literature and contributes to interdisciplinary programs on peace and Chinese philosophy. His translations and studies span numerous Chinese classics—from philosophy and poetry to myth and fantasy—including major works on ancient Chinese mythology such as “Mitología clásica china” and “Mitología de la China antigua,” as well as anthologies of Tang and Song tales and popular ancient Chinese poetry. Through these and many other translations of classical texts, he has significantly widened access to Chinese intellectual and literary traditions in the Spanish-speaking world.

Beyond his academic role, García-Noblejas has been active in building institutional and cultural bridges between China and Spain. He is a cofounder of the Spanish Association of Teachers for Chinese Language Instruction and a member of Cátedra China, and he frequently participates in public lectures and essays that interpret Chinese classics for contemporary audiences. His work emphasizes translation as a vehicle of mutual understanding, seeking to strengthen cultural and even political relations by fostering a deeper appreciation of China’s literary and philosophical heritage.
