## Biography
Gabriel García-Noblejas Sánchez-Cendal (1966– ) is a Spanish scholar, translator, and professor specializing in Chinese literature and Chinese–Spanish translation. Born in Oviedo in 1966, he first studied Spanish Philology in his home city before continuing his education abroad in Durham and Pau, and later in Beijing, where he undertook intensive studies in Chinese language and civilization at several universities in the 1990s. He subsequently completed a doctorate on the translation of Chinese literature into Spanish and has since taught Chinese literature, translation, and Chinese–Spanish interpreting at the University of Granada, where he has also offered widely attended cross‑disciplinary courses on the great works of world culture and on ancient Chinese conceptions of peace.citeturn0search4turn0search3

As a bridge between Chinese and Spanish cultures, García-Noblejas has translated numerous Chinese classics, including foundational works of philosophy, religion, and poetry, and has edited major bilingual editions such as the "Libro de los cantos" (Book of Odes). He is also the author of influential studies on Chinese mythology, notably "Mitología clásica china" and related works that synthesize textual, archaeological, and artistic sources for a Spanish‑speaking audience.citeturn0search2turn0search6 His collaborations have extended to other languages and traditions—for example, a joint translation of Rif Berber tales with Zoubida Boughaba—and institutionally he has co‑founded the Spanish Association of Teachers for the Teaching of Chinese and served on the advisory council of Cátedra China, reinforcing his role as a key figure in shaping contemporary Spanish understanding of China.citeturn0search1turn0search5
