## Biography
Peter John Rhodes (1940–2021), commonly cited as P. J. Rhodes, was a British historian of ancient Greece whose work reshaped modern understanding of classical Greek politics and institutions. Educated at Queen Elizabeth’s School, Barnet, and Wadham College, Oxford, he completed a doctorate on the Athenian council (boule) and quickly established himself as a leading scholar of Athenian democracy. His long career was based at the University of Durham, where he taught from the mid‑1960s, serving as Professor of Ancient History and later as Honorary and Emeritus Professor.

Rhodes became internationally known for meticulous studies of Greek political life, combining close reading of literary texts with epigraphic and institutional analysis. Among his many books, works such as *The Athenian Boule*, his commentary on the *Athenaion Politeia*, and synthetic surveys like *A History of the Classical Greek World, 478–323 BC* made complex scholarship accessible to students and specialists alike, and helped frame how subsequent generations approached the politics and culture of classical Greece. His reputation brought invitations and visiting fellowships at leading institutions abroad, and he played an active role in scholarly organizations.

Over his career Rhodes received numerous honors, including election as a Fellow of the British Academy and membership in foreign academies, reflecting his standing within the international classics community. He was also involved in mentoring younger scholars and collaborating widely, leaving an extensive body of publications that continues to influence the study of ancient democracy and Greek political history. He died in Durham in 2021, remembered as both a rigorous scholar and a generous colleague.
