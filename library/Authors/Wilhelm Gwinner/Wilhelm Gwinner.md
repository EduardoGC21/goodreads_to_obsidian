## Biography
Wilhelm Robert Franz Gwinner (1825–1917), later ennobled as Wilhelm von Gwinner, was a German jurist and man of letters best known as the first major biographer of the philosopher Arthur Schopenhauer. Born on October 17, 1825, in Frankfurt am Main to Philipp Friedrich Gwinner and Catharina von Rolland, he received a classical education at the Frankfurt Gymnasium before studying law at the universities of Jena and Heidelberg. After earning his doctorate in law in 1847, he practiced as an advocate in his native city and entered the legal service of the Free City of Frankfurt, eventually serving as secretary of the appellate court and later as a city court judge.

Alongside his legal career, Gwinner pursued philosophy and theology studies in Tübingen and cultivated a deep intellectual interest in contemporary thought. This background prepared him for his close relationship with Arthur Schopenhauer, who spent his final decades in Frankfurt. Gwinner became one of Schopenhauer’s trusted confidants, visiting him frequently, witnessing his daily habits and moods, and being present shortly before the philosopher’s death. Named executor of Schopenhauer’s will, he was responsible both for administering the philosopher’s estate and for preserving his intellectual legacy.

Gwinner’s lasting significance lies in his detailed portrayals of Schopenhauer’s life and circle of friends. Works such as “Arthur Schopenhauer aus persönlichem Umgang dargestellt” and the expanded biography “Schopenhauers Leben” draw heavily on personal acquaintance, letters, and recollections, providing an intimate picture of Schopenhauer’s character, relationships, and reception in 19th‑century Germany. The Spanish edition “Arthur Schopenhauer presentado desde el trato personal: Una mirada a su vida, su carácter y su pensamiento” continues this tradition by making Gwinner’s perspective accessible to new audiences. Gwinner died in Frankfurt on January 27, 1917, and was buried in the family grave at the city’s main cemetery, remembered primarily as Schopenhauer’s close friend, executor, and authoritative early biographer.
