## Biography
Kentaro Miura (1966–2021) was a Japanese manga artist best known as the creator of the dark fantasy epic "Berserk." Born in Chiba Prefecture, he began drawing manga as a child and debuted professionally in his teens, quickly demonstrating a meticulous draftsmanship and flair for complex, emotionally charged storytelling. After studying at Nihon University’s College of Art and briefly working as an assistant to established creators, Miura launched "Berserk" in 1989. The series, with its blend of medieval brutality, psychological depth, and philosophical themes, followed the warrior Guts and became one of the most influential seinen manga of all time, inspiring anime, novels, and video games and shaping the tone of modern dark fantasy worldwide.

Over more than three decades, Miura’s reputation rested not only on the narrative ambition of "Berserk" but also on his extraordinary artistic detail and willingness to explore trauma, friendship, betrayal, and the human will to persist under overwhelming darkness. Later volumes such as "Berserk, Vol. 37" through "Berserk Volume 41" reflected his ongoing evolution as an artist and storyteller, as he deepened the series’ emotional and mythological scope. Miura worked closely with a small studio (Studio Gaga) and built lasting professional relationships with editors and assistants who helped sustain the demanding production schedule. His death in 2021 from acute aortic dissection was widely mourned by readers, fellow artists, and creators across media, cementing his legacy as one of the most significant and influential manga authors of his generation.
