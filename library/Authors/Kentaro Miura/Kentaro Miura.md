## Biography
Kentaro Miura (1966–2021) was a Japanese manga artist best known as the creator of the dark fantasy series *Berserk*. Born in Chiba, Japan, he began drawing manga at a very young age and produced his first works while still in school, quickly showing an unusual command of anatomy, composition, and intricate detail. After studying at Nihon University’s art program, he continued refining *Berserk*, which debuted in the late 1980s and became his life’s central project.

Miura’s work on *Berserk*—including later volumes such as those in the user’s library—earned him a reputation for combining visceral, often brutal storytelling with philosophical and psychological depth. He drew on European medieval imagery, classical literature, and religious symbolism to explore trauma, ambition, loyalty, and the consequences of power through characters like Guts and Griffith. Despite the series’ long serialization schedule and frequent breaks, readers remained devoted, and his meticulous artwork influenced many manga artists, animators, and game creators worldwide.

Known to be private and soft‑spoken, Miura maintained close working relationships with his editors and assistants, and was respected in the manga industry for his discipline and perfectionism. His sudden death in 2021 left *Berserk* unfinished and prompted tributes from fans and fellow creators across the globe, highlighting his profound impact on dark fantasy storytelling and on the visual language of modern manga and games.
