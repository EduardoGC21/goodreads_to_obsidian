## Biography
Robert Malcolm Errington (born 5 July 1939 in Howdon-on-Tyne, England) is a British historian and classical scholar specializing in ancient Greek and Roman history. Educated at Durham University, where he studied Classics from 1958 to 1963 and completed his PhD in Ancient History in 1966, he went on to an academic career that took him from Queen’s University Belfast, where he taught from 1966 to 1973, to the Philipps-Universität Marburg in Germany, where he served as Professor of Ancient History until his retirement and later held the title of emeritus professor.

Errington’s research has focused on the political structures of the Greek city-states, the history of Macedonia, the Hellenistic monarchies, Roman expansion, Roman–Greek relations, Roman legal history, and Late Antique Christianity. His major works include studies such as *Philopoemen*, *A History of Macedonia*, and *Roman Imperial Policy from Julian to Theodosius*, as well as *A History of the Hellenistic World: 323–30 BC*, a widely used survey that synthesizes the complex political history of the post-Alexandrian era. He has been closely involved with epigraphic scholarship and served from 2001 to 2007 as project leader of the *Inscriptiones Graecae*, reflecting his commitment to integrating inscriptions into historical interpretation.

Throughout his career Errington has been part of an international community of scholars, holding visiting memberships at institutions such as the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, and he is a full member of the German Archaeological Institute. As a teacher and supervisor he has influenced a generation of historians of the ancient world, particularly in Germany and the United Kingdom, helping to shape modern understanding of the Greek and Roman worlds and their transition from the Classical and Hellenistic periods into Late Antiquity.
