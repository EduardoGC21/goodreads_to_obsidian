## Biography
Jackson W. Crawford (born 1985) is an American scholar, translator, and educator specializing in Old Norse language, literature, and mythology. Raised in the United States and trained as a linguist and Scandinavianist, he earned a B.A. in Classics from Texas Tech University, an M.A. in Linguistics from the University of Georgia, and a Ph.D. in Scandinavian Studies from the University of Wisconsin–Madison. He went on to teach Old Norse, Norse mythology, and the history of the Scandinavian languages at the University of California, Los Angeles; the University of California, Berkeley; and the University of Colorado Boulder, where he also coordinated the Nordic program.

Crawford is best known outside academia for his role as a public communicator of Norse culture. Through his widely followed YouTube channel and his accessible modern-English translations of key medieval texts, he has sought to present Old Norse sources in a clear, historically grounded way, separate from both pop‑culture fantasy and esoteric or mystical appropriations. His translation of "The Poetic Edda: Stories of the Norse Gods and Heroes" exemplifies this approach, prioritizing readability and fidelity to the myths for general readers while drawing on his scholarly expertise. He has also consulted on major films, video games, and other media projects involving Norse language and runes, extending his influence beyond the classroom.

Crawford’s public persona, often framed by the landscapes of the American West and a self-described “cowboy” sensibility, emphasizes independence, clarity, and respect for primary sources. This combination of regional identity and philological rigor has made him an unusual figure who bridges academic scholarship and popular interest, shaping how many contemporary readers and viewers encounter Norse myth and the Old Norse language.
