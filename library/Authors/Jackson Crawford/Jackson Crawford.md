## Biography
Jackson Crawford (1985–) is an American scholar and translator of Old Norse language and literature, known for making the medieval Scandinavian world accessible to general audiences. Born in the United States, he pursued advanced studies in linguistics and Old Norse, and has taught at institutions including the University of Colorado Boulder, the University of Wyoming, and UCLA, where he focused on historical linguistics, runology, and Norse mythological texts.

Crawford’s significance lies in his clear, idiomatic English translations and his pedagogical approach, which emphasizes linguistic accuracy without sacrificing readability. His translation of “The Poetic Edda: Stories of the Norse Gods and Heroes” and other works on Norse sagas have become popular both in academic settings and among enthusiasts of mythology, literature, and Viking-age history. Through these publications he has helped reframe Old Norse sources as living literature rather than esoteric specialist material.

Beyond print scholarship, Crawford is widely recognized for his educational outreach, especially via online videos and public lectures explaining Old Norse language, runes, and mythology in a rigorous yet approachable style. This combination of academic training, translation work, and broad public engagement has made him one of the most visible contemporary interpreters of Norse tradition and its cultural context.
