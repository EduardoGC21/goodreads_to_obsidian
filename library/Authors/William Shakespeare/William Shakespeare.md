## Biography
William Shakespeare (1564–1616) was an English playwright, poet, and actor born in Stratford-upon-Avon. The son of a glove-maker, John Shakespeare, and Mary Arden, he received a grammar school education before marrying Anne Hathaway in 1582; together they had three children, including a son, Hamnet, whose early death is often seen as a shadow over Shakespeare’s later tragedies. By the early 1590s, Shakespeare had established himself in London as a working dramatist and performer, eventually becoming a shareholder in the Lord Chamberlain’s Men (later the King’s Men), the company that performed many of his plays at the Globe and Blackfriars theatres.

Writing in the vibrant context of Elizabethan and Jacobean England, Shakespeare created comedies, histories, and tragedies that explore power, love, ambition, and identity, including works such as "Hamlet," "Romeo and Juliet," "Macbeth," and "The Merchant of Venice". His close ties to a professional acting company and to patrons at court helped shape his drama for both popular and elite audiences. Shakespeare’s innovative use of English, psychologically complex characters, and insight into human relationships and political life have made him a central figure in world literature, and his works remain among the most performed and studied texts in the Western canon.
