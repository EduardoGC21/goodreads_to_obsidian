## Biography
Francisco García Bazán (1940–2025) was an Argentine philosopher, historian of religions, and university professor whose work reshaped modern understanding of early Christianity, ancient philosophy, and Gnosticism. Born in Málaga, Spain, on March 27, 1940, he emigrated to Argentina in his teens and later became a naturalized Argentine citizen. He studied philosophy at the University of Buenos Aires, graduating in 1968 with highest honors, and earned his PhD in Philosophy in 1975 from the Universidad del Salvador, also summa cum laude. Between 1971 and 1974 he was a CONICET fellow in Buenos Aires and at the Pontifical Gregorian University in Rome, where he studied under the Jesuit scholar Antonio Orbe. Over his long career he rose to the rank of Investigador Superior at CONICET and held teaching posts at the University of Buenos Aires, the Universidad del Salvador, and the Universidad Argentina John F. Kennedy, where he also served as dean of philosophy and directed the Center for Research in Philosophy and History of Religions as well as the journal "Epimelia."
