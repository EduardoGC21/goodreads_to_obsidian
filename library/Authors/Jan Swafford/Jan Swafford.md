## Biography
Jan Swafford (born 1946 in Chattanooga, Tennessee) is an American composer, biographer, and writer whose work has helped shape modern understanding of the Western classical tradition. Trained at Harvard and the Yale School of Music, he studied with figures such as Earl Kim, Jacob Druckman, and Betsy Jolas before emerging as a distinctive voice in contemporary composition, often described as lyrically expressive and rooted in a kind of modern classicism that balances clarity, emotion, and structural rigor. Alongside his composing career he taught composition, theory, music history, and writing at institutions including the Boston Conservatory and Tufts University, while remaining closely tied to the musical life of Boston and New England.

Swafford is widely known for his major biographies of Charles Ives, Johannes Brahms, Ludwig van Beethoven, and Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, as well as his accessible guides to classical music. His Beethoven biography, for example, brought together his skills as analyst, historian, and storyteller to present the composer’s life in its social and psychological context, and it reached a broad readership beyond specialists. His books have garnered honors such as a National Book Critics Circle nomination, the PEN/Winship Award, and a Deems Taylor Award for music writing, and they are valued for combining archival research with a vivid sense of character and place.

Beyond books, Swafford has been an influential commentator and educator through program notes, journalism, and broadcasting. He has written extensively for orchestras such as the Boston Symphony, Cleveland Orchestra, Chicago Symphony, and others, providing audiences with lucid and often deeply human portraits of composers and works. His essays and appearances on outlets like Slate, NPR, and the BBC have made him a recognizable public voice on classical music, bridging the worlds of scholarship, performance, and general readership while maintaining a strong personal connection to the American musical landscape that formed him.
