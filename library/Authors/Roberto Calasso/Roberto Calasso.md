## Biography
Roberto Calasso (1941–2021) was an Italian writer, publisher, and cultural critic whose work bridged myth, literature, religion, and philosophy. Born in Florence, he grew up in an intellectual milieu—his father was a law professor active in the anti-fascist resistance—which shaped his early fascination with classical studies and comparative culture. Calasso studied English literature at the University of Rome, but his true vocation emerged as a reader and interpreter of the world’s mythological and literary traditions, which he wove into highly original, genre-defying books.

Calasso’s international reputation rests on works such as "Ka" (on Indian myth), "Literature and the Gods," and "Las bodas de Cadmo y Harmonía" ("The Marriage of Cadmus and Harmony"), where he reimagined foundational stories from Hindu, Greek, and European traditions as living forces within modern consciousness. At the same time, he was a central figure in European publishing as the long-time head and later owner of Adelphi Edizioni, one of Italy’s most prestigious literary houses. There he championed and curated an influential catalogue that brought major foreign and Italian authors to a wide readership. Known for his erudition, stylistic refinement, and refusal to separate scholarship from imaginative writing, Calasso became a key reference point for late 20th- and early 21st-century reflections on myth, modernity, and the fate of literature.
