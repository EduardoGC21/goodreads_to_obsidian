## Biography
Leo Tolstoy (1828–1910) was a Russian novelist, moral philosopher, and social critic whose work reshaped modern literature and religious thought. Born into an aristocratic family in Yasnaya Polyana, he experienced privilege alongside loss and instability in childhood, which fed his lifelong concern with inequality and moral responsibility. After university studies and service as an army officer, including time at Sevastopol during the Crimean War, he drew on his experiences of violence, fear, and heroism to write some of his earliest celebrated stories.

Tolstoy achieved international fame with the vast historical novel "War and Peace," which used the Napoleonic Wars to explore fate, free will, and the lives of both aristocrats and commoners. Later works like "The Death of Ivan Ilyich" deepened his preoccupation with spiritual crisis, the fear of death, and the search for authentic living. In midlife he underwent a profound religious and ethical conversion, rejecting much of his wealth, criticizing church and state, and advocating for nonviolence, simple living, and the dignity of peasants. His radical moral stance inspired figures such as Mahatma Gandhi and Martin Luther King Jr., and his combination of psychological insight, social critique, and spiritual inquiry has made him one of the central figures in world literature.

Tolstoy’s personal life was marked by tension between his ideals and his responsibilities as a husband and father of thirteen children. His marriage to Sofia (Sonya) Behrs was intense and often conflict-ridden, especially as he tried to renounce property and literary rights that sustained the family. Yet Yasnaya Polyana remained a creative and intellectual hub, where he wrote, taught peasant children, and received visitors from around the world. His final years were spent in increasing estrangement from his aristocratic roots and in pursuit of a purer moral life, a quest that ended dramatically when he fell ill and died at a remote railway station while attempting to leave home in secrecy.
