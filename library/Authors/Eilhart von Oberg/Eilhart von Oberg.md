## Biography
Eilhart von Oberg (often recorded as Eilhart von Oberge) was a German poet active in the late 12th century and associated with the northern German noble family from the estate of Oberg, between Braunschweig and Hildesheim. Very little is known with certainty about his life, but he is generally placed in the milieu of the Saxon and Welf courts, possibly as a ministerial or retainer connected to Henry the Lion or his son, the future emperor Otto IV. Modern scholarship suggests that his cultural horizon lay in regions where French influence was strong, and that he had access to French narrative poetry circulating among the aristocracy.
