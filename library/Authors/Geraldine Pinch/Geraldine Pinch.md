## Biography
Geraldine Pinch (often credited in fiction as Geraldine Harris) is a British Egyptologist and author born in 1951. Trained at both Oxford and Cambridge, she has specialized in the religion, magic, and mythology of ancient Egypt, and has taught Egyptology and related courses at the University of Oxford as well as at Cambridge. Over her career she has been associated with Oxford’s Oriental Institute and has served on the councils and editorial boards of leading scholarly bodies, including the Egypt Exploration Society, the Journal of Egyptian Archaeology, and later the Journal of Ancient Near Eastern Religions, reflecting her standing within the field.

Pinch’s academic work is notable for making complex religious and mythological material accessible without sacrificing rigor. In studies such as “Magic in Ancient Egypt,” “Handbook of Egyptian Mythology,” and “Egyptian Myth: A Very Short Introduction,” she pieced together fragmentary textual and visual evidence to reconstruct how ancient Egyptians understood their gods, myths, and ritual practices. “Egyptian Mythology: A Guide to the Gods, Goddesses, and Traditions of Ancient Egypt” exemplifies her approach: it offers a comprehensive yet readable survey that has become a standard reference for students and general readers interested in Egyptian beliefs.

Alongside her scholarly publications, Pinch has written for wider audiences, including children and fantasy readers, sometimes under her maiden name Harris. This dual authorship has allowed her to bridge academic research and imaginative literature, introducing generations of readers to ancient Egypt through both carefully documented studies and works of fiction. Her career is marked less by public controversy than by steady, influential contributions that clarify the religious and mythological world of ancient Egypt for specialists and non-specialists alike.
