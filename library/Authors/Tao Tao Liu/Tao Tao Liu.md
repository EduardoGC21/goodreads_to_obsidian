## Biography
Tao Tao Liu is a Chinese-born scholar of Chinese literature and religion who built her academic career in the United Kingdom. Trained in classical and modern Chinese studies, she became known for her ability to present traditional narratives and religious ideas to non‑specialist readers without losing their historical depth or cultural nuance. Over the course of her career she specialized in the interplay between myth, religion, and literature in China, helping to contextualize stories and deities within broader philosophical and social developments.

Liu spent much of her professional life at the University of Oxford, where she served as a senior lecturer in Chinese literature in the Faculty of Oriental Studies and was associated with Wadham College. In this role she taught generations of students, introducing them both to canonical texts and to lesser‑known religious and mythological traditions. Her book on Chinese myths, published in English for a general audience, exemplifies her broader contribution: it not only retells key legends but situates them within Chinese religious practice, festivals, and everyday beliefs, thereby making China’s mythic heritage accessible to readers around the world.

Now retired from her Oxford post, Liu remains recognized as an expert interpreter of Chinese mythology and religion. Her work stands out for bridging academic research and public understanding, offering readers a way into complex traditions shaped by Daoism, Buddhism, and Confucianism while preserving the narrative power and religious significance of the myths themselves.
