## Biography
Tao Tao Liu is a scholar of Chinese literature and mythology, best known in the English-speaking world for her accessible introductions to the rich narrative traditions of China. A retired senior lecturer in Chinese literature in the Faculty of Oriental Studies at the University of Oxford and an Emeritus Fellow of Wadham College, she has spent much of her career teaching Chinese literary history and cultivating cross-cultural understanding of China’s classical texts and stories.

Her work brings together literary analysis, religious history, and cultural anthropology, showing how myths and legends illuminate everyday practices, festivals, and popular beliefs in China. In books such as *The Chinese Myths: A Guide to the Gods and Legends* (also published in Spanish as *Los mitos chinos: Una guía de sus dioses y leyendas*), Liu presents figures like creator deities, demigods, dragons, and bodhisattvas not only as characters in ancient tales but as living presences in ritual and imagination. Through her research, translations, and teaching, she has helped shape how contemporary readers outside China encounter and appreciate Chinese mythological and literary traditions.

Liu’s work is also closely tied to the legacy of modern Chinese literary scholarship and translation, including contributions to studies honoring major translators of Chinese classics. Combining careful philology with an engaging narrative style, she has become a trusted mediator between Chinese-language sources and international audiences, influencing students, general readers, and specialists interested in the interplay between myth, religion, and literature in Chinese culture.
