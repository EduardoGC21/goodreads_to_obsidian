## Biography
Matt Manero is an American entrepreneur, author, and speaker best known for his work on financial independence and business culture. In 1995 he founded Commercial Fleet Financing, Inc. (CFF) in Dallas, starting with a folding table, a phone, and a single trucking client. Over the following decades he built CFF into one of the largest independently owned transportation equipment finance companies in the United States, funding over a billion dollars in equipment and earning repeated recognition on the Inc. 500/5000 lists of fastest-growing companies. He later became CEO of multiple related companies, positioning himself as a “blue‑collar” business leader serving truckers and small-business owners.

Manero’s writing and media work extend his entrepreneurial message. He first wrote “The Grit,” then published “You Need More Money: Wake Up and Solve Your Financial Problems Once And For All,” where he draws on his own early struggles and the wake‑up call of his brother‑in‑law’s death to argue for radical financial responsibility and wealth building as a path to freedom. He has hosted a nationally syndicated radio show and podcasts, delivered TEDx talks, and spoken widely at business groups and conferences, emphasizing themes of personal accountability, goal setting, and strong organizational culture.

In his personal life, Manero is based in the Dallas, Texas area with his wife and three sons. His public persona centers on being a dedicated husband and father as well as a mentor to employees and entrepreneurs. Through his companies, books, and speaking, he aims to help others overcome complacency, design lives of financial security, and create workplaces that combine high performance with gratitude and meaningful opportunity for employees.
