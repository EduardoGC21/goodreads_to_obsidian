## Biography
Barbara C. Morden is a British cultural historian, biographer, and lecturer whose work focuses on eighteenth- and nineteenth-century art and literature, as well as key figures in modern British painting. Born and educated in Newcastle upon Tyne, she developed a strong regional and historical awareness that informs her writing, particularly in her attention to the social, religious, and intellectual contexts in which artists worked. She has been affiliated with the Open University in England as a research consultant and lecturer in eighteenth- and nineteenth-century arts, and has also served as a visiting lecturer in art history at the University of Durham, roles that have allowed her to mentor students and contribute to public understanding of art history in the UK and beyond.

Morden’s biography of the Romantic painter John Martin, "John Martin: Apocalypse Now!", exemplifies her approach: she reconstructs the artist’s Northumbrian origins, his London career, and his international reputation to show how his apocalyptic biblical scenes resonated with contemporary culture and later visual media. Alongside this study, she has written on the poet-physician Mark Akenside and the painter Laura Knight, extending her interests across literature, medicine, and women’s art. Through these works, lectures, and conference papers in both the UK and the USA, Morden has helped to reframe canonical and marginal figures alike, emphasizing their broader cultural significance and the ways in which their lives and images continue to shape modern perceptions of history, gender, and visual storytelling.
