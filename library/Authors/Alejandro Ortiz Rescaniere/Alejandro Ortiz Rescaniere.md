## Biography
José Alejandro Ortiz Rescaniere (d. 2024) was a Peruvian anthropologist and linguist renowned for his pioneering work on Andean cosmologies, indigenous mythologies, and the lived experiences of Quechua- and Aymara-speaking communities. Over more than three decades as a principal professor of anthropology at the Pontificia Universidad Católica del Perú, he trained generations of students, helped found the journal "Anthropologica," and directed the university’s master’s program in anthropology, becoming a central figure in the institutional consolidation of social anthropology in Peru.﻿([departamento-ciencias-sociales.pucp.edu.pe](https://departamento-ciencias-sociales.pucp.edu.pe/noticias/fallecimiento-de-nuestro-profesor-jose-alejandro-ortiz-rescaniere?utm_source=openai))

Early in his career, Ortiz Rescaniere explored formal approaches to language in works such as "Set theory and linguistics" (1968), before turning fully to ethnographic and mythological research grounded in the Andean world.﻿([openlibrary.org](https://openlibrary.org/books/OL5610294M/Set_theory_and_linguistics.?utm_source=openai)) A disciple of José María Arguedas and Claude Lévi-Strauss, he combined structural analysis with a deep commitment to indigenous voices, collecting and interpreting myths from multiple Peruvian regions in books like "De Adaneva a Inkarri. Una visión indígena del Perú," which highlighted the unity and historical depth of Andean culture.﻿([lugareditorial.com.ar](https://lugareditorial.com.ar/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/Educacion_Intercultural_Bilingue.pdf?utm_source=openai)) Through studies on figures such as Inkarri, on marriage and cosmic change, and on Andean notions of the individual, he offered influential readings of how myth, ritual, and everyday life articulate power, identity, and memory in the highlands.﻿([revistas.pucp.edu.pe](https://revistas.pucp.edu.pe/index.php/anthropologica/article/view/1012?utm_source=openai))

Ortiz Rescaniere maintained close intellectual and personal ties with Arguedas’s circle, later editing materials that documented that milieu and its impact on Peruvian thought.﻿([iberlibro.com](https://www.iberlibro.com/9789972420566/Arguedas-recuerdos-amistad-9972420566/plp?utm_source=openai)) As editor of "Mitologías amerindias," he also helped situate Andean traditions within a broader panorama of Amerindian religious and mythological systems, fostering dialogue between Latin American and international scholarship.﻿([si.edu](https://www.si.edu/object/mitologias-amerindias-edicion-de-alejandro-ortiz-rescaniere-johannes-neurath-et-al%3Asiris_sil_895542?utm_source=openai)) His mentorship shaped the work of younger anthropologists, including Juan Javier Rivera Andía, and his writings continue to serve as a bridge between indigenous oral traditions and rigorous academic analysis, cementing his stature as one of the key interpreters of the Andean world in the late twentieth and early twenty-first centuries.﻿([rumbosur.org](https://www.rumbosur.org/ethnographica/canaris2/?utm_source=openai))
