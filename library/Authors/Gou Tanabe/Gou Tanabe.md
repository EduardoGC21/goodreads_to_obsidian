## Biography
Gou Tanabe is a Japanese manga artist born in 1975 in Tokyo, in Japan’s Kantō region. Emerging in the early 2000s, he first drew attention with original works and literary adaptations that showcased a subdued, meticulous visual style and a strong interest in psychological tension. His breakthrough came when an editor introduced him to H. P. Lovecraft’s Cthulhu Mythos during a difficult creative period; Tanabe was struck by Lovecraft’s doomed protagonists who “lose all hope and appetite for life,” a sensibility that resonated with his own storytelling interests.

From that encounter, Tanabe developed a career largely defined by ambitious, faithful yet distinctive manga adaptations of Lovecraft’s fiction, including "At the Mountains of Madness" and other major tales. His work is notable for dense, atmospheric artwork, careful pacing, and an emphasis on human vulnerability in the face of incomprehensible cosmic forces. Widely translated and recognized abroad, Tanabe has helped bring Lovecraft’s universe to new audiences and has been praised by peers such as Junji Ito for effectively claiming this niche of literary horror adaptation. Beyond Lovecraft, he has also adapted and illustrated other writers’ works, reinforcing his reputation as a specialist in thoughtful, visually rich literary horror manga.
