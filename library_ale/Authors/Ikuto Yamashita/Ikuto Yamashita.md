## Biography
Ikuto Yamashita (born 1965 in Gifu Prefecture, Japan) is a Japanese manga artist and mechanical designer best known for defining the look of Neon Genesis Evangelion. After studying at Nagoya University of Arts, he began working in manga and animation, developing a reputation for intricate, organic mecha designs that blurred the line between machine and living being. His manga work includes the science‑fiction series Dark Whisper, while in animation he served as lead mecha designer on Evangelion, co‑creating the iconic Evangelion units and much of the series’ mechanical and symbolic visual language.

Beyond Evangelion, Yamashita has contributed mechanical designs to a wide range of anime and related media, including Gunbuster, Nadia: The Secret of Blue Water, Blue Submarine No. 6, Sentō Yōsei Yukikaze, and later projects such as the Rebuild of Evangelion films and various video games. As the primary creator and supervisor of Neon Genesis Evangelion: ANIMA, he extended the franchise’s world and technology in prose and illustration, showcasing his distinctive vision of “enormous power restrained.” His work has had a lasting influence on mecha design in anime, inspiring later creators with its fusion of realism, body horror, and mythic imagery.

Yamashita is known to keep a relatively low public profile, continuing to live and work in Gifu Prefecture while collaborating with directors and studios on new projects. His professional relationships with figures such as Evangelion director Hideaki Anno and various Gainax and Studio Khara staff have anchored him at the center of late‑20th‑ and early‑21st‑century mecha design, making him one of the key artists behind the evolution of the genre.
