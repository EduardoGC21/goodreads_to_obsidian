## Biography
James Smith Dashner (born 1972) is an American novelist best known for writing speculative fiction for children and young adults, particularly the bestselling Maze Runner and Mortality Doctrine series. Born in Austell, Georgia, and raised in a large family, he developed an early fascination with reading and storytelling, typing his own tales on his parents’ typewriter. After graduating from Duluth High School in 1991, he moved to Utah to attend Brigham Young University, where he earned a master’s degree in accounting and later worked in finance before turning to writing full-time.

Dashner’s breakthrough came with series fiction that blended high-stakes adventure, survival, and science‑fiction concepts, culminating in the global success of The Maze Runner books and their film adaptations, which firmly established him as a major figure in contemporary YA genre fiction. He continued to expand his body of work with series such as The 13th Reality and The Mortality Doctrine, including titles like “The Eye of Minds,” “The Rule of Thoughts,” and “The Game of Lives,” and later branched into adult horror with “The House of Tongues.”

He has lived for many years in Utah, where he and his wife, Lynette Anderson, are raising four children. In 2018, his career was significantly affected when allegations of sexual misconduct led to the loss of his agent and major publisher; Dashner publicly apologized and stated that he would seek counseling, marking a controversial chapter in an otherwise commercially successful career.
