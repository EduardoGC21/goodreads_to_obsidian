## Biography
Eiren Caffall is a writer and musician whose work centers on loss, nature, oceans, and extinction, often exploring how people live, grieve, and imagine futures in the midst of ecological crisis. Based in Chicago, she has published essays and criticism in venues such as Guernica, The Los Angeles Review of Books, Literary Hub, Al Jazeera, The Rumpus, and the Center for Humans and Nature, and she has contributed writing to collaborative art and photography projects as well as three record albums. Her practice spans literature, music, and film, including writing for the short film "Becoming Ocean."

Caffall’s books extend this focus on environmental and intimate catastrophe. Her memoir "The Mourner’s Bestiary" and her novel "All the Water in the World" draw on her deep interest in the oceans, extinction, and the emotional lives of people facing planetary and personal collapse, weaving together scientific awareness, family experience, and a strong sense of place. Her work has been recognized with a Whiting Foundation Creative Nonfiction Grant, a Social Justice News Nexus fellowship at Northwestern University, and residencies at artist and writer communities such as the Banff Centre, Hedgebrook, Ragdale, and others.

Alongside her public achievements, Caffall writes out of a life shaped by chronic illness and inherited genetic disease, themes that inform her reflections on vulnerability, care, and resilience. She continues to live and work in Chicago with her family, contributing to contemporary conversations about climate change, mourning, and the possibilities for love, community, and artistic witness in a time of environmental upheaval.
