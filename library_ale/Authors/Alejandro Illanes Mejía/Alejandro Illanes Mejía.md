## Biography
Alejandro Illanes Mejía is a Mexican mathematician, professor, and science communicator whose career has been closely linked to the Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México (UNAM). He completed his undergraduate, master’s, and doctoral studies in mathematics at UNAM, later joining the Faculty of Sciences as a professor and the Institute of Mathematics as a researcher. His main area of research is topology, particularly hyperspaces and continuum theory, where he has authored dozens of research papers and co‑authored specialized monographs that have become standard references for other researchers in the field.

Alongside his research, Illanes has devoted much of his energy to teaching and the popularization of mathematics. He has taught numerous undergraduate and graduate courses, supervised many theses at all levels, and received the prestigious Universidad Nacional award in the area of teaching in exact sciences in recognition of his pedagogical work. His interest in outreach has led him to give talks in high schools, universities in Mexico and abroad, and to participate actively in the organization of the Mexican Mathematics Olympiads, helping to identify and nurture young mathematical talent.

Illanes has also written expository and popular science books, including “La caprichosa forma de Globión,” in which he uses an imaginative narrative about the shape of a fictional planet to introduce readers to the mathematical theory of surfaces and topology. Through this and other works, he has sought to demystify mathematics and present it as a creative, visually appealing discipline, bringing advanced ideas within the reach of a broad audience while maintaining rigorous standards of clarity and precision.
