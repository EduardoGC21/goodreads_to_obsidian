## Biography
Baoshu (pen name of Li Jun, born 1980 in Guangyuan, Sichuan Province, China) is a leading figure in the new generation of Chinese science fiction and fantasy writers. Trained as a philosopher, he earned master’s degrees in philosophy from Peking University and from Katholieke Universiteit Leuven before turning to writing full time in 2012. His early engagement with online bulletin boards and fan communities strongly shaped his voice, and he first gained widespread attention with a fan-written continuation of Liu Cixin’s Remembrance of Earth’s Past trilogy, later published with Liu’s support as Three Body X and translated into English as The Redemption of Time, which helped launch his professional career.

Over the following years Baoshu became one of the most decorated contemporary Chinese SF authors, winning multiple Nebula Awards for Science Fiction and Fantasy in Chinese and Galaxy Awards for Chinese Science Fiction. His body of work ranges from large-scale cosmic sagas to time-loop and apocalyptic narratives such as Ruins of Time, and his stories have appeared in influential venues and anthologies, contributing to the global recognition of Chinese speculative fiction. In addition to writing, he has edited thematic SF anthologies and translated major works, including Olaf Stapledon’s Star Maker, further cementing his role as a bridge between Chinese and international science fiction traditions.

Baoshu maintains close professional relationships within the Chinese SF community, notably with Liu Cixin and fellow writers who emerged from similar online circles, and he is a member of the Science Fiction Committee of the Chinese Writers Association. His works have been translated into multiple languages and he has held international fellowships, reflecting both his domestic stature and his growing influence abroad. He continues to live and work in China, developing fiction that combines philosophical inquiry with imaginative speculation about humanity’s future.
