## Biography
Orest Stelmach is a Ukrainian-American novelist and former investment professional whose life bridges immigrant roots, global experience, and commercial literary success. Born in Connecticut to parents who emigrated from Ukraine, he grew up in a tight-knit Ukrainian-American community and spoke Ukrainian before learning English, later becoming fluent in Spanish and Japanese as well. He studied at Dartmouth College and the University of Chicago Booth School of Business, then spent decades managing large international investment portfolios after earlier stints doing hands-on jobs and teaching English in Japan.

Stelmach eventually turned full-time to writing, drawing heavily on his heritage and on the geopolitical landscapes he had come to know. His thrillers, including the Nadia Tesla series that begins with “The Boy from Reactor 4,” explore Ukrainian identity, the legacy of Chernobyl, and the moral ambiguities of global finance and power, often through strong, complex protagonists navigating international intrigue. His books have become Kindle bestsellers, been translated into multiple languages, and optioned for film, extending his influence well beyond the Ukrainian diaspora.

He lives in Connecticut with his wife and a rescue cat, remaining closely connected to his cultural background while engaging readers worldwide through fast-paced stories that weave together family history, political trauma, and scientific and economic themes.
