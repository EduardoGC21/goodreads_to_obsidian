## Biography
H.A. Swain is an American author of speculative fiction and children’s literature whose work often explores technology, consumer culture, and the emotional lives of young people. Writing under the form H.A. Swain for her young adult dystopian novels and as Heather Swain for picture books, she has created stories that range from futuristic thrillers to gentle explorations of love and family. Her YA novel "Hungry" imagines a world where food and hunger have been engineered away, using that premise to examine privilege, resistance, and bodily autonomy, while its companion thriller "Gifted" continues her interest in the ethics of purchased talent and social inequality.

Beyond her young adult work, Swain has written picture books such as "All Kinds of Kisses" and "How Many Hugs," which focus on affection, connection, and early childhood relationships, demonstrating her range from dark speculative worlds to tender family themes. She lives with her family in Brooklyn, New York, where her experiences as a parent and city-dweller inform the emotional grounding and social awareness of her stories. Though detailed public information about her early life and career trajectory is limited, her body of work has established her as a contemporary voice in YA dystopian fiction and children’s books, using imaginative premises to probe how technology and power shape human connection.
