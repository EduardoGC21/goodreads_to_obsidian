## Biography
Barbara Minto (b. 1930) is an American-born consultant, author, and former McKinsey & Company associate best known for developing the Minto Pyramid Principle, a structured approach to clear, logical communication. Trained at the Harvard Business School and later joining McKinsey as one of its early female consultants, she became deeply involved in improving how consultants organized and presented complex ideas. Her work culminated in “The Pyramid Principle: Logic in Writing and Thinking,” which codified her method for structuring arguments from top-level messages down through supporting points.

After leaving McKinsey, Minto founded her own training and coaching practice, working with corporations, professional service firms, and executives around the world to sharpen their written and oral communication. Her approach has had a lasting impact on management consulting, business writing, and executive education, and the “pyramid” structure she popularized is now widely taught as a standard method for organizing thinking and presentations. She is recognized primarily for this contribution to the craft of analytical communication rather than for a broad literary output.

Minto’s career is also significant in the context of women’s increasing participation in high-level business roles in the latter half of the 20th century. As one of relatively few women in elite consulting during her early career, she helped model a path for later generations, not only through her client work but through her teaching and mentoring on how to present ideas persuasively and logically in demanding professional environments.
