## Biography
Sun Tzu (also transliterated Sunzi) is the reputed author of *The Art of War*, a foundational treatise on military strategy and statecraft that has shaped thinking on conflict for more than two millennia. Traditionally described as a Chinese military commander and strategist serving a ruler of the state of Wu during the Spring and Autumn period, he was credited with distilling practical battlefield experience and political insight into concise maxims about leadership, discipline, intelligence, deception, and the efficient use of force.

Because he is a semi-legendary figure, the precise details of Sun Tzu’s life, including his exact dates, birthplace, and specific military campaigns, remain uncertain and debated among historians. Some scholars view him as a single historical individual, while others see "Sun Tzu" as a composite or symbolic authorial name representing a school of strategic thought. Regardless of his exact identity, the work attributed to him became highly influential within imperial China, guiding generals, officials, and scholars in matters of war, diplomacy, and governance.

Over centuries, *The Art of War* traveled far beyond its original context, shaping military doctrine in East Asia and later being studied globally by soldiers, political leaders, and thinkers in fields as diverse as business and negotiation. Sun Tzu’s enduring significance lies in his emphasis on understanding human motives, careful planning, psychological insight, and achieving victory with minimal destruction, ideas that continue to resonate in modern discussions of strategy and leadership.
