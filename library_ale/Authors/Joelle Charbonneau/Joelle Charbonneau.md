## Biography
Joelle Charbonneau (born 1974 near Chicago, Illinois) is an American author best known for her young adult novels, including the dystopian Testing trilogy. Trained in music and classical voice, she earned an undergraduate degree in music and a master’s in opera performance before spending her early career as a professional singer and actress in opera and musical theatre around the Chicagoland area. Her background as a performer led her to work as a private voice instructor, a role she has continued alongside her writing career.

Charbonneau transitioned from the stage to the page by writing mysteries and later young adult fiction for children and teens. She has written multiple series, including the Rebecca Robbins skating mysteries, the Glee Club mysteries, and several bestselling YA works such as The Testing, Independent Study, and Graduation Day, as well as Dividing Eden and other standalone novels. Her books have achieved New York Times and USA Today bestseller status and have appeared on recommended and award lists for young readers, helping to shape contemporary YA genre fiction with stories that explore high-stakes choices, ethics, and social pressures.

She lives in the Chicago area with her family and continues to balance teaching voice with a prolific writing schedule. Through her career, Charbonneau has been noted for drawing on her performing-arts background and for creating accessible, fast-paced stories that appeal to both avid and reluctant young readers.
