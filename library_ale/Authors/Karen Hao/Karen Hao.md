## Biography
Karen Hao is an American journalist and data scientist whose work has become central to public understanding of artificial intelligence and its social consequences. Raised bilingual in English and Mandarin, she studied mechanical engineering and energy studies at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology before briefly working as an application engineer at a startup spun out of Google X. She then shifted into journalism and data visualization, reporting for Quartz and later joining MIT Technology Review, where she became senior editor for AI, launched the influential newsletter The Algorithm, and co-produced the award‑winning podcast In Machines We Trust.

Hao’s reporting has focused on how AI systems intersect with power, labor, the environment, and democracy, from exposing tensions inside platforms like Facebook over misinformation control to documenting the hidden human and ecological costs of large‑scale AI. Her work has appeared in outlets including The Atlantic, The Wall Street Journal, and other major publications, and has been cited in policy debates, academic courses, and museum exhibitions. She also helped create the Pulitzer Center’s AI Spotlight Series, which has trained journalists worldwide to cover AI accountability.

In 2025 she published “Empire of AI: Dreams and Nightmares in Sam Altman’s OpenAI,” a deeply reported account of OpenAI and the global race to build powerful AI systems. Drawing on extensive interviews and global field reporting, the book situates OpenAI’s rise within a broader story about concentrated corporate power, colonial patterns of resource extraction, and the contested future of AI, cementing Hao’s reputation as one of the leading critical voices on the technology.
