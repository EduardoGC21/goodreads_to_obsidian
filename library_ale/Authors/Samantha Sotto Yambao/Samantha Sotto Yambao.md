## Biography
Samantha Sotto Yambao is a Filipino speculative fiction author whose work blends fantasy, romance, and magical realism. Born and raised in Manila, she moved to the Netherlands as a teenager, where she studied marketing at Webster University’s Leiden campus and fell in love with Europe’s old architecture and layered histories. She later returned to the Philippines, completed an AB Communications degree at Ateneo de Manila University, and worked for nearly a decade in brand management before turning her attention to writing.

Her breakthrough came when she began drafting her debut novel, “Before Ever After,” during hours spent in coffee shops while waiting for her son after school—a routine that transformed a full-time mother into an internationally published author. She has since written several novels, including “Love and Gravity,” “A Dream of Trees,” “The Beginning of Always,” and the fantasy “Water Moon,” building a body of work known for imaginative premises, time-bending narratives, and emotionally resonant love stories. Now a Sunday Times, USA Today, Indie List, and New York Times bestselling author, she lives in Manila with her husband, children, and their golden retriever, and continues to write whimsical, character-driven stories that explore love, regret, and second chances across time and reality.

Sotto Yambao’s significance lies in helping bring Filipino voices into the international speculative fiction space while maintaining a distinctly personal and emotionally intimate style. Drawing on her experiences of travel, motherhood, and cross-cultural life between Europe and the Philippines, she often writes about characters who are dislocated in time or place yet searching for connection and meaning. Her success with books like “Water Moon,” which extends her signature blend of cozy fantasy and magical realism to a wider global audience, has made her a notable contemporary figure in genre-bending romantic fantasy.
