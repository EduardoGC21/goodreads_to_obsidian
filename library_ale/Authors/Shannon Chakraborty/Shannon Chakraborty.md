## Biography
Shannon Ali Chakraborty (publishing as Shannon Chakraborty and formerly as S. A. Chakraborty) is an American author of historical fantasy and speculative fiction, born in New Jersey in 1985. Raised in a Catholic family, she converted to Islam in her teens and originally planned an academic career as a historian of the Middle East before the 2008 financial crisis pushed her toward other work. During this period she began writing what she called “historical fan fiction” set in an intricately researched, Islamicate fantasy world—stories that eventually became her acclaimed debut novel, The City of Brass, and the rest of the Daevabad Trilogy, which brought her international recognition and multiple award nominations.

Chakraborty’s fiction is noted for its blend of myth, history, and politics, as well as its focus on faith, family, and morally complicated characters. Her later work, including the swashbuckling historical fantasy The Adventures of Amina al-Sirafi, continues to explore themes of motherhood, religious identity, and the legacies of empire through adventurous, character-driven narratives. She lives in New Jersey with her husband and children, and her books have been translated into many languages, making her one of the most prominent contemporary voices in epic fantasy inspired by Middle Eastern and Indian Ocean histories.
