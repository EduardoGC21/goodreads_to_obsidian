## Biography
Alberto Clemente de la Torre is an Argentine physicist and science popularizer whose work bridges rigorous quantum theory and broad humanistic inquiry. He studied engineering and applied physics in Córdoba, Argentina, and later continued his education at Drexel University before earning a doctorate in theoretical physics from the University of Heidelberg in Germany. During some fifteen years in Germany he combined research with teaching at Heidelberg and at the universities of Hamburg and Wuppertal, consolidating a profile that links deep technical expertise with a strong didactic vocation.

Since 1986 De la Torre has lived in Mar del Plata, Argentina, where he has devoted himself both to the study of quantum physics and to phenomenological research on elementary particles. Alongside this specialized work, he has become known for books that present modern physics to non-specialists in clear, concept‑centered language, including "Física cuántica para filo-sofos" and later works such as "Física en perspectiva humanística" and "Universo sin dioses." Through these publications he has sought to make the conceptual and philosophical implications of contemporary physics accessible to readers far beyond the physics classroom, positioning himself as a significant figure in Spanish-language scientific outreach.

De la Torre’s career illustrates a sustained effort to connect the abstract formalism of quantum mechanics with broader cultural and philosophical questions. His teaching and writing emphasize interpretation, critical reflection, and the role of physics in shaping our understanding of reality, appealing both to students of science and to intellectually curious readers from the humanities. In this way he has contributed to a richer dialogue between scientific practice and humanistic concerns in Latin America and the wider Spanish-speaking world.
