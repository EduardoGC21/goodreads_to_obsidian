## Biography
Reki Kawahara is a Japanese light novel author best known for creating the hugely popular science-fiction and fantasy series "Sword Art Online" (SAO) and "Accel World." Born in 1974 in Gunma Prefecture, he originally self‑published "Sword Art Online" as a web novel before submitting "Accel World" to the 15th Dengeki Novel Prize in 2008, where it won the Grand Prize and launched his professional writing career. His work quickly became a pillar of the light novel boom of the late 2000s and 2010s, shaping how virtual reality and online game worlds were depicted in Japanese pop culture.

Kawahara’s stories often explore the psychological and social impacts of immersive virtual environments, focusing on themes such as identity, trauma, friendship, and romance under extreme conditions. Volumes like the "Phantom Bullet" and "Mother’s Rosario" arcs of "Sword Art Online" are notable for expanding the emotional and thematic range of the series, tackling issues like PTSD, chronic illness, and loss alongside high‑stakes action. His characters and settings have inspired a vast media franchise, including anime, manga, games, and films, making him one of the most internationally recognized light novel authors of his generation.

Despite his prominence, Kawahara keeps much of his private life out of the public eye, allowing his creative output to define his reputation. He continues to supervise and expand the worlds he created, influencing not only subsequent light novel authors but also broader discussions of virtual reality and online communities in fiction.
