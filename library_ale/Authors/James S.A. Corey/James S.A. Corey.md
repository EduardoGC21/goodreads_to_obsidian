## Biography
James S.A. Corey is the shared pen name of American science fiction writers Daniel Abraham and Ty Franck, who joined forces to create large‑scale, character‑driven space opera. Both were longtime genre professionals—Abraham as a novelist and short‑story writer, Franck as an assistant and collaborator to George R.R. Martin—before deciding to pool their strengths under a single pseudonym. The “S.A.” in the name comes from the initials of Abraham’s daughter, and the unified persona allows them to present a single creative voice despite being a duo.

Their collaboration is best known for The Expanse, beginning with *Leviathan Wakes*, which helped revitalize modern space opera through its blend of hard‑science plausibility, political intrigue, and ensemble storytelling. The series grew into a multi‑volume saga and spawned an acclaimed television adaptation, establishing “James S.A. Corey” as a major name in contemporary science fiction. Working jointly on novels, shorter fiction, and related projects, Abraham and Franck have continued to explore questions of power, technology, and human community on an interplanetary and galactic scale.

Beyond The Expanse, the duo has written tie‑in work such as a *Star Wars* novel and has launched new projects like the Captive’s War universe, extending their influence across different parts of the genre. Their partnership is notable for its durability and for the way it merges two distinct careers into a single, consistent authorial identity that has become a touchstone for readers seeking expansive, socially grounded SF.
