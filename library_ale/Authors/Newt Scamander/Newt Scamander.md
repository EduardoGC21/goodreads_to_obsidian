## Biography
Newt Scamander is a fictional British magizoologist from J.K. Rowling’s Wizarding World, best known as the in‑universe author of the Hogwarts textbook *Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them*. Born in 1897, he develops a passionate interest in magical creatures from a young age, encouraged in part by his mother’s Hippogriff breeding. Trained at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, he later joins the Ministry of Magic, initially working in the Office for House‑Elf Relocation before moving to the Beast Division, where his expertise with magical fauna truly flourishes.

Scamander’s most significant achievement is his extensive research and fieldwork on magical creatures across the globe, cataloguing their behavior, habitats, and the ethics of their treatment. This work culminates in the publication of *Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them*, a standard reference at Hogwarts (including the Spanish edition, *Animales fantásticos y dónde encontrarlos*). His life and travels place him in contact with important figures in the wizarding world and embroil him in major conflicts of the early 20th century, particularly those involving dark wizard Gellert Grindelwald. Within the fictional canon, Newt Scamander is portrayed as a gentle, introverted, and principled figure whose legacy shapes magical education and attitudes toward magical creatures for generations.
