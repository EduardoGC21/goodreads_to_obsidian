## Biography
Marie Lu (born Xiwei Lu, 1984) is a Chinese-born American author of young adult science fiction and fantasy, best known for the dystopian Legend series, including the novels Legend and Prodigy. Born in Wuxi, Jiangsu, China, she spent part of her early childhood in Beijing before emigrating with her family to Texas in 1989 during the political upheaval surrounding the Tiananmen Square protests. Growing up in the American South, she learned English in part through writing stories, an early sign of the narrative imagination that would shape her career.

Lu studied political science and biology at the University of Southern California and initially pursued a career in the video game industry, interning and later working as an artist and art director for Disney Interactive Studios and related projects. This visual and interactive background informs the cinematic pacing and worldbuilding of her novels, which often blend action, technology, and morally complex characters. With the publication of Legend in 2011, she quickly became a prominent voice in young adult literature, followed by numerous bestsellers across series such as The Young Elites, Warcross, and later works that expanded her audience.

Now a full-time writer, Lu lives in Los Angeles, California, with her husband and their child. Her work is noted for centering young people thrust into extraordinary circumstances, exploring themes of power, loyalty, and rebellion against oppressive systems. She has become an influential figure in contemporary YA fiction, particularly as a Chinese-American woman writing blockbuster genre series that resonate with readers worldwide.
