## Biography
Simon Jimenez (b. 1989) is a Filipino-American writer of speculative fiction whose work blends intimate human relationships with grand, formally experimental narratives. Raised between Canada, the Philippines, and later the United States, he grew up immersed in science fiction, an enthusiasm he credits in part to his father. Jimenez studied creative writing at Emerson College, where he completed an MFA and began developing the voice that would define his later fiction.

His debut novel, The Vanished Birds (2020), established him as a major new talent in science fiction, earning nominations for the Locus Award for Best First Novel, the Arthur C. Clarke Award, and the British Fantasy Award, as well as a finalist spot for the Astounding Award for Best New Writer. With his second novel, The Spear Cuts Through Water (2022), he turned to mythic fantasy, using an inventive narrative structure to explore themes of storytelling, colonial violence, queer identity, and family; the book went on to win the British Fantasy Award and the IAFA Crawford Award and to be shortlisted for several further prizes. Critics frequently praise his lyrical prose, complex structures, and focus on chosen families, marginalized characters, and the emotional textures of displacement.

Openly gay and of mixed Filipino heritage, Jimenez often weaves questions of belonging, diaspora, and queer experience into his work, depicting characters whose bonds are tested across time, distance, and oppressive regimes. He continues to live and write in the United States, contributing to a rising generation of genre authors who use speculative frameworks to interrogate history, memory, and identity while pushing stylistic boundaries.
