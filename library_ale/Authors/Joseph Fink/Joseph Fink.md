## Biography
Joseph Fink is an American writer and audio creator best known as the co-creator of the cult podcast "Welcome to Night Vale" and the creator of the horror road-trip podcast "Alice Isn’t Dead." Emerging from the early boom of narrative audio drama, he helped define a distinctive blend of surreal small-town Americana, dark humor, and emotional sincerity that attracted a large international audience.

Building on his podcast success, Fink has become a New York Times bestselling author, co‑writing novels such as "Welcome to Night Vale," "It Devours!," and "The Faceless Old Woman Who Secretly Lives in Your Home" with longtime collaborator Jeffrey Cranor, and writing prose works like the novelization of "Alice Isn’t Dead" and the middle‑grade book "The Halloween Moon." With his wife, writer and performer Meg Bashwiner, he co‑authored the memoir "The First Ten Years," reflecting on their relationship and creative life together. Fink has lived in New York, the Hudson River Valley, and later California, continuing to develop genre‑bending fiction that bridges podcasting and print while influencing a generation of contemporary audio storytellers.
