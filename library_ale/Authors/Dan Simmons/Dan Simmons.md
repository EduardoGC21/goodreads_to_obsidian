## Biography
Dan Simmons (1948–2026) was an American novelist best known for combining science fiction, horror, fantasy, and historical fiction into ambitious, genre‑defying works. Born in Peoria, Illinois, he studied English at Wabash College and earned a master’s in education from Washington University in St. Louis before spending 18 years as an elementary school teacher in Missouri, New York, and Colorado, where he helped design programs for gifted students and was a finalist for Colorado Teacher of the Year. His storytelling gifts were evident in the classroom, where he famously spun an ongoing epic tale for his students that later helped inspire the universe of the Hyperion Cantos.

Simmons’s literary career took off with the World Fantasy Award–winning novel “Song of Kali” (1985), but he achieved global recognition with “Hyperion” (1989) and its sequels “The Fall of Hyperion,” “Endymion,” and “The Rise of Endymion,” which blended space opera with rich literary and philosophical allusion. Over the course of more than three decades he also wrote acclaimed works such as “Carrion Comfort,” “The Terror,” and numerous thrillers and short stories, winning major honors including the Hugo, World Fantasy, Bram Stoker, Shirley Jackson, and multiple Locus awards. He lived for many years in Colorado with his wife, Karen, and their daughter, Jane, and remained active as a writer until his death in Longmont, Colorado, in 2026.

Within contemporary speculative fiction, Simmons is regarded as a central figure who expanded the thematic and stylistic range of the field. His work is noted for its intricate structures, literary intertextuality, and willingness to tackle questions of faith, mortality, violence, and history, influencing a generation of later genre writers. Adaptations such as the television series based on “The Terror” further cemented his reputation beyond the core science fiction and horror readership, and posthumous tributes from fellow authors have emphasized both his technical mastery and his originality across genres.
