## Biography
Ernest Christy Cline (born 1972) is an American novelist, screenwriter, and former slam poet whose work blends science fiction with an encyclopedic love of late‑20th‑century pop culture. Raised in Ashland, Ohio, he spent his youth immersed in video games, science‑fiction films such as Back to the Future and Star Wars, and tabletop role‑playing games, obsessions that later became the imaginative fuel for his fiction. Before publishing fiction, he worked various support and IT jobs while honing his writing, and gained a following on the performance poetry circuit in Austin, Texas, where his humorous, geek‑centric spoken‑word pieces circulated widely online.

Cline first broke into Hollywood by co‑writing the screenplay for the cult film “Fanboys,” a comedy about Star Wars devotees. His debut novel, “Ready Player One,” turned his lifelong engagement with gaming and pop culture into a bestseller and cultural touchstone, later adapted into a major film for which he co‑wrote the script. He followed it with other speculative works, including “Armada,” further cementing his reputation for stories that celebrate and interrogate fandom, nostalgia, and virtual worlds. Cline continues to live in Austin with his family, a customized DeLorean inspired by his beloved films, and an extensive collection of classic video games, remaining an influential figure at the intersection of science‑fiction literature, fan culture, and contemporary media.
