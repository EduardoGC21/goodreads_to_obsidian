## Biography
Roberto Calasso (1941–2021) was an Italian writer, editor, and publisher whose work explored the deep connections between myth and modern consciousness. Born in Florence into a family of academics, philosophers, and publishers, he grew up surrounded by books and intellectual debate. His father, a legal historian and antifascist, was arrested and condemned to death during the Second World War, forcing the family into hiding and marking Calasso’s early years with political danger as well as scholarly rigor.

After studying English literature in Rome, Calasso joined the nascent publishing house Adelphi Edizioni in the early 1960s and spent his life there, eventually becoming its guiding editor and later owner. Under his leadership Adelphi became one of Europe’s most distinctive literary publishers, known for a catalog that combined rigorous scholarship with visionary, often heterodox literature and philosophy. His own multi-volume, genre-defying oeuvre—of which “Ka,” a reimagining of Hindu myth, is a major installment—wove together mythology, religion, literature, and history into a single expansive meditation on how stories shape human awareness.

Calasso’s personal life intertwined with the literary world: he married the Swiss novelist Fleur Jaeggy and maintained close relationships with writers and intellectuals across Europe. Celebrated as a “literary institution of one,” he received international recognition for sustaining a uniquely erudite and ambitious body of work while also curating the voices of others as an editor. He died in Milan in 2021, leaving behind a legacy as both a singular mythographer of modernity and one of Italy’s most influential publishers.
