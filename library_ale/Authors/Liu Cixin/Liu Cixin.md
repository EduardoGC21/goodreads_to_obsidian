## Biography
Liu Cixin (1963–) is a Chinese science fiction writer whose work has been central to the global rise of contemporary Chinese SF. Born in Yangquan, Shanxi Province, he studied engineering and spent many years working as a computer engineer at a power plant, an experience that shaped his grounded, technically informed approach to speculative ideas. Liu began publishing science fiction in Chinese magazines in the 1990s and quickly became one of the leading voices of the genre in China.

Liu’s international breakthrough came with the "Remembrance of Earth’s Past" trilogy, beginning with "The Three-Body Problem" and continuing through "The Dark Forest" and "Death’s End", in which he blends hard science, large-scale cosmic speculation, and reflections on history and politics. The English translation of "The Three-Body Problem" won the Hugo Award for Best Novel in 2015, making Liu the first Asian author to receive that honor and significantly expanding the global readership for Chinese science fiction. His work has influenced both Chinese and international writers, and he is widely regarded as a major figure in modern SF for the ambition, scientific rigor, and philosophical depth of his stories.
