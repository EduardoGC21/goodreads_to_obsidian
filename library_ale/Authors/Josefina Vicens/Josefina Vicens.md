## Biography
Josefina Vicens (1911–1988) was a Mexican novelist, screenwriter, journalist, and political activist whose small body of fiction had an outsized impact on twentieth‑century Mexican literature. Born in Villahermosa, Tabasco, to a Spanish father and Mexican mother, she moved with her family to Mexico City as a child and early on learned office skills that allowed her to support herself independently, an important goal in a period when few women held public roles. Her work in government offices and factories exposed her to social and economic inequalities, experiences that fueled a lifelong commitment to farmers’ rights, women’s rights, and social justice.

Alongside her political activism in agrarian and labor organizations, Vicens built a prolific career as a journalist and screenwriter, often publishing under pseudonyms such as Pepe Faroles and Diógenes García. She wrote dozens of screenplays and became an influential figure in Mexican cinema, eventually serving as president of the Mexican Academy of Cinematographic Sciences and Arts and advocating for better conditions and recognition for screenwriters. In her personal life she cultivated close relationships with artists and writers in mid‑century Mexico City’s intellectual circles, while also insisting on her own autonomy within marriage and work.

Vicens’s literary reputation rests especially on her two introspective novels, “El libro vacío” (1958) and “Los años falsos” (1982), works that explore themes of authorship, identity, and the tension between ordinary life and creative ambition. “El libro vacío” was awarded the prestigious Xavier Villaurrutia Prize, making her one of the few women of her generation to receive such recognition. In her final years she struggled with the loss of her eyesight but remained intellectually engaged until her death in Mexico City in 1988, remembered as a fiercely independent voice who bridged literature, film, and political engagement.
