## Biography
Ken Liu (born 1976) is a Chinese American author, translator, and former technology and law professional whose work has significantly shaped contemporary science fiction and fantasy. Born in Lanzhou, Gansu, China, he spent much of his early childhood with his grandparents before immigrating to the United States at age eleven. Growing up in California and Connecticut in a family of scientists and engineers, he developed a strong interest in both storytelling and technology. He studied English literature and computer science at Harvard College, later earning a J.D. from Harvard Law School, and worked as a software engineer, corporate lawyer, and high‑tech litigation consultant before writing full time.

Liu rose to prominence through short fiction published in major speculative magazines and anthologies. His story “The Paper Menagerie,” collected in *The Paper Menagerie and Other Stories*, became the first work of fiction to win the Hugo, Nebula, and World Fantasy Awards, marking him as a central figure in 21st‑century speculative fiction. He went on to write the epic “silkpunk” fantasy series *The Dandelion Dynasty*, which blends East Asian historical influences with engineering‑driven worldbuilding, and has received multiple Locus and Nebula recognitions. Alongside his original fiction, Liu is highly regarded for bringing Chinese science fiction to Anglophone readers, most notably through his award‑winning English translation of Liu Cixin’s *The Three‑Body Problem* and other translated works that helped spark global interest in Chinese SF.

Liu’s career is also marked by close collaboration with his family: his wife, Lisa Tang Liu, has co‑written fiction with him, and he often credits his grandmother for inspiring his love of storytelling. Now based in the United States, he continues to write novels, short stories, and essays, advise on adaptations of his work for film and television, and serve as a cultural bridge between Chinese and Western speculative traditions.
