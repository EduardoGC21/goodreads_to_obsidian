## Biography
Christopher Paolini (1983– ) is an American fantasy author best known for creating the Inheritance Cycle, beginning with the novel "Eragon". Homeschooled in Montana, he wrote "Eragon" as a teenager, and his family initially self-published the book before it was picked up by a major publisher. Its success turned Paolini into one of the youngest bestselling authors of all time, and he quickly became a prominent figure in contemporary young adult fantasy.

Paolini’s work on "Eragon," followed by "Eldest," "Brisingr," and "Inheritance," is notable for its expansive secondary world, blending classic fantasy influences like dragons, magic, and ancient races with a coming-of-age story. The rapid success of the series drew attention to both his youth and his disciplined writing process, as he spent years revising and expanding the narrative and mythology of Alagaësia. Beyond commercial success, his trajectory has inspired many young writers, particularly those interested in epic fantasy.

Paolini grew up in a close-knit family that played a central role in his early career, with his parents handling much of the early editing, design, and promotion of "Eragon." His life and work reflect a mix of homespun, family-supported beginnings and global literary fame, situating him as an example of how nontraditional educational paths and independent publishing can lead to mainstream recognition.
