## Biography
Amal El-Mohtar (1984– ) is a Canadian writer, poet, and critic of Lebanese descent whose work has become highly influential in contemporary science fiction and fantasy. Born in Ottawa and spending part of her childhood in Lebanon, she grew up between cultures, a background that informs the rich, folkloric and lyrical textures of her fiction and poetry. She later pursued advanced studies in English literature and has taught creative writing at Canadian universities, mentoring new generations of speculative writers.

El-Mohtar first gained wide recognition through her short fiction and poetry, winning major genre awards for pieces such as “Seasons of Glass and Iron.” As co-author, with Max Gladstone, of the novella "This Is How You Lose the Time War," she achieved international acclaim; the book became a New York Times bestseller and a multiple award winner, cementing her reputation as a leading voice in speculative fiction. Alongside her creative work, she serves as a prominent critic and essayist, contributing long-running columns and reviews on science fiction and fantasy to venues such as The New York Times and NPR, and she has also edited the influential poetry magazine Goblin Fruit, helping to shape the field’s taste for imaginative, boundary-pushing work.

Her career is marked not only by awards and critical recognition but also by her active engagement with literary communities and readers, from teaching and podcast appearances to festival and convention participation. She lives in Ottawa and continues to write across forms—fiction, poetry, and criticism—exploring themes of time, love, myth, and language in ways that resonate with both genre audiences and the broader literary world.
