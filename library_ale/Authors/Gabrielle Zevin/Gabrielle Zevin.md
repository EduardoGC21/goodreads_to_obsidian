## Biography
Gabrielle Zevin (1977– ) is an American novelist and screenwriter whose work often explores identity, memory, and the power of stories. Born in New York City to a father of Eastern European Jewish descent and a Korean-born mother, she grew up in Boca Raton, Florida, as an only child before studying English and American literature at Harvard University. Her mixed cultural background and bookish upbringing helped shape her interest in how people reinvent themselves across different lives, places, and even media.

Zevin began publishing fiction and writing for film in the early 2000s, gaining attention with the novel "Elsewhere" and the screenplay for "Conversations with Other Women," which earned an Independent Spirit Award nomination. She has since written for both adults and young readers, including the international bestseller "The Storied Life of A.J. Fikry" and the later hit "Tomorrow, and Tomorrow, and Tomorrow," which cemented her reputation for emotionally rich, formally inventive storytelling. Many of her books have been widely translated, underscoring her global readership.

Over her career, Zevin has maintained close creative and personal ties with filmmaker Hans Canosa, her long-term partner and collaborator on several screen projects. After more than a decade in New York City, she relocated to Los Angeles, where she continues to write novels and screenplays that bridge popular appeal with literary depth, particularly in their nuanced portrayals of friendship, love, and creative partnership.
