## Biography
Eliezer Braun (born 1949 in Mexico City) is a Mexican physicist and science educator whose work has focused on making contemporary ideas in physics and mathematics accessible to a broad audience. He studied Physics at the Faculty of Sciences of the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM), and later completed a doctorate in Mathematics and Natural Sciences at the Lorentz Institute of Leiden University in the Netherlands. His research and teaching have spanned fields such as statistical and theoretical physics, nonlinear phenomena, and the emerging theories of chaos and fractals that transformed scientific thinking in the late twentieth century.

Throughout his career, Braun has held academic and research positions at major institutions in Mexico and Europe, including UNAM and the Universidad Autónoma Metropolitana (UAM) Iztapalapa, where he has served as a full‑time professor. He has also worked in research centers such as the Nuclear Center of Mexico’s reactor facilities and has been a visiting researcher in laboratories abroad. These roles have placed him at the intersection of advanced scientific research and university teaching, earning him recognition as a distinguished professor at UAM and as an influential voice in the Mexican scientific community.

Braun is best known to the general public for his popular science writing, particularly his book "Caos, fractales y cosas raras," in which he introduces readers to the concepts of chaos theory and fractal geometry and their applications in diverse disciplines. By presenting complex ideas with clarity and an emphasis on real-world phenomena, he has contributed to expanding scientific literacy in the Spanish-speaking world and to situating modern physics and mathematics within a broader cultural and intellectual context.
