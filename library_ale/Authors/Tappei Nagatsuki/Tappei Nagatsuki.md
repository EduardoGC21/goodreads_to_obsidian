## Biography
Tappei Nagatsuki (born 1987) is a Japanese novelist best known as the creator of the light novel series “Re:Zero − Starting Life in Another World.” He first gained attention by serializing Re:Zero as a web novel on the user-generated fiction site Shōsetsuka ni Narō under the pen name Nezumi-iro Neko, building a substantial online readership before the series was acquired for print publication. His work is noted for its intricate plotting, psychological intensity, and willingness to subject characters to severe emotional and moral trials.

After Re:Zero moved into print, it expanded into a major multimedia franchise, including manga adaptations, multiple anime seasons, and video games, with Nagatsuki remaining closely involved in the adaptations through script meetings and production supervision. Alongside Re:Zero, he has contributed to or created other projects such as the mixed-media series “Warlords of Sigrdrifa” and the sci‑fi anime and novel project “Vivy: Fluorite Eye’s Song,” further cementing his place in contemporary Japanese pop culture.

Nagatsuki is part of a generation of light novel authors who emerged from web fiction platforms and often collaborates or interacts publicly with peers known for isekai and fantasy series. Through works like “Re:ZERO -Starting Life in Another World-, Vol. 1,” he has reached an international audience, influencing both light novels and anime storytelling with his blend of high-stakes fantasy, character-driven drama, and meticulous worldbuilding.
