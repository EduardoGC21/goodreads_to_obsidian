## Biography
Walter Moers (born 1957) is a German comic artist, illustrator, and novelist whose work ranges from sharp, often provocative satire to expansive, literary fantasy. Raised in Mönchengladbach, West Germany, he left school early and supported himself through odd jobs before gradually turning his self-taught drawing skills into a profession. He first gained national attention in the 1980s and 1990s with comics for German satire magazines, creating controversial and darkly humorous characters that challenged political correctness and social norms.

Alongside this adult-oriented work, Moers developed children’s stories and characters, most notably Captain Bluebear, who became famous through television, books, and audio plays. Building on this success, he reinvented himself as an internationally known fantasy author with the Zamonia novels, such as "The 13½ Lives of Captain Bluebear" and "The City of Dreaming Books", which blend grotesque humor, literary allusions, elaborate world-building, and his own illustrations. These books have earned him a wide readership among both adults and younger readers and established him as a distinctive voice in contemporary German-language fantasy.

Moers is known for his extreme privacy: he rarely appears in public, seldom grants interviews except by email, and allows few photographs to circulate, contributing to his reputation as a “phantom” of German literature. He has lived in Hamburg since the early 1990s and continues to expand the fictional continent of Zamonia, influencing comic art, fantasy literature, and German popular culture with his idiosyncratic mix of satire, imagination, and visual storytelling.
