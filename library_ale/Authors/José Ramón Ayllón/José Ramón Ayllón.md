## Biography
José Ramón Ayllón (born 1955 in Cantabria, Spain) is a Spanish philosopher, educator, and writer whose work bridges academic thought and accessible cultural commentary. Trained in Philosophy and Letters at the universities of Oviedo and Valladolid, and with further specialization in bioethics, he has combined university teaching with an extensive career as an essayist, novelist, and textbook author. Over the years he has taught anthropology, ethics, literature, and philosophy in secondary schools and at institutions such as the University of Navarra and other universities, becoming known as a clear, didactic communicator on questions of ethics, education, and the human person.

Ayllón’s essays explore the ideological and cultural currents of the contemporary West, moral education, and the foundations of European civilization, while his biographies and studies of figures like G. K. Chesterton and Sophie Scholl have contributed to renewing interest in 20th‑century Christian and humanist thinkers. At the same time, his novels, including youth fiction such as "Vigo es Vivaldi", "Diario de Paula" and "Otoño azul", use engaging stories to introduce young readers to classical culture, ethical dilemmas, and questions of meaning. Through books, lectures, and media appearances he has become a widely recognized public intellectual in the Spanish‑speaking world, appreciated for connecting philosophical reflection with everyday life and education.

Beyond his published work, Ayllón has been active as a lecturer and consultant on values education, addressing themes such as conscience formation, emotional intelligence, friendship, and family life. His combination of academic rigor, narrative talent, and pedagogical experience has made him an influential voice for educators, parents, and students interested in a humanistic approach to culture and ethics.
