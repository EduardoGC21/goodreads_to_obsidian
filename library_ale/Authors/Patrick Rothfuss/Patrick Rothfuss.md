## Biography
Patrick James Rothfuss (born 1973) is an American fantasy author best known for The Kingkiller Chronicle, a planned trilogy that begins with The Name of the Wind and continues with The Wise Man’s Fear. Born in Madison, Wisconsin, he grew up as an avid reader, encouraged by parents who fostered his imagination in part by limiting television. His long and meandering time at the University of Wisconsin–Stevens Point, where he eventually earned a degree in English, helped shape both his love of storytelling and the fictional university life that features so prominently in his novels.

After completing a master’s degree in English at Washington State University, Rothfuss returned to Wisconsin, teaching at his alma mater while revising the massive manuscript that would become The Kingkiller Chronicle. Early recognition came when an excerpt, “The Road to Levenshir,” won the Writers of the Future contest, and his debut novel later garnered major awards and bestseller status, with The Wise Man’s Fear reaching the top of The New York Times list. His meticulous worldbuilding, lyrical prose, and focus on character-driven fantasy have made him a central figure in contemporary epic fantasy.

Beyond his fiction, Rothfuss is known for his close relationship with fans and for founding the charity Worldbuilders, which leverages the speculative-fiction community to raise funds for causes such as Heifer International. He lives in Wisconsin with his family, dividing his time between writing, gaming and role-playing projects, and ongoing philanthropic work connected to his books and the broader fantasy community.
