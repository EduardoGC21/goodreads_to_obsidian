## Biography
Claire Fraise is an American author of young adult speculative fiction and paranormal thrillers, noted for combining fast‑paced plots with emotionally rich character work. Homeschooled in Wilton, Connecticut, she began drafting her debut novel, the dystopian YA thriller "Imperfect," as a high school freshman and published it at the age of sixteen. The book’s focus on relationships under extreme pressure foreshadowed her later interest in the psychological dimensions of fear and resilience.

After the success of "Imperfect," Fraise continued to build a career writing eerie, supernatural stories for teen and crossover audiences, eventually moving into paranormal horror and thriller series fiction. She has become best known for her ghost‑hunting novels, including the award‑winning "They Stay" series, which won the Grand Prize in the 2023 Writer’s Digest Self‑Published Book Awards and further established her as a leading independent voice in YA paranormal suspense. Alongside her fiction, she is active in the broader writing community, sharing craft advice through her “Write with Claire Fraise” YouTube channel and maintaining a direct connection with readers via social media and events.

Fraise has lived in both Connecticut and Massachusetts, studying at Tufts University in Boston while continuing to publish. Away from the page she is an avid reader and outdoors enthusiast who enjoys hiking, horseback riding, and spending time with her rescue dogs—personal passions that often inform the close-knit relationships and themes of loyalty and courage that run through her work.
