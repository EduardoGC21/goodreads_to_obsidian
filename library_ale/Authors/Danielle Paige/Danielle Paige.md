## Biography
Danielle Paige is an American author best known for her young adult fantasy series that reimagines classic stories with darker, subversive twists. Before becoming a novelist, she worked as a television writer, which helped shape her talent for fast-paced plotting, episodic tension, and character-driven drama. She later transitioned to prose, quickly gaining attention in the YA community for her cinematic style and high-concept premises.

Her breakout novel, "Dorothy Must Die," launches a series that recasts the world of Oz as a corrupt, dystopian realm and explores themes of power, morality, and identity through a new protagonist drawn into the conflict. Paige’s work often centers teenage girls placed at the crux of mythic or fantastical upheaval, highlighting questions of choice, loyalty, and self-definition. Active in contemporary YA circles, she has contributed to the ongoing trend of reinventing familiar tales for modern readers, helping bridge pop culture, classic literature, and young adult fantasy.

Beyond her signature series, Paige has written additional YA projects and collaborated on multi-author initiatives, extending her reach across different corners of speculative fiction for teens. Her career reflects a blend of media and narrative forms, with her background in television informing the vivid, episodic structure of her novels. She remains a recognizable voice in 21st-century YA fantasy, particularly for readers drawn to reimagined worlds and heroines navigating moral gray areas.
