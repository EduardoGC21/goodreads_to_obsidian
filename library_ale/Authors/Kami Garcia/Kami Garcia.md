## Biography
Kami Garcia (born 1972 in Washington, D.C.) is an American novelist and comic book writer best known for co‑creating the bestselling young adult fantasy series *Beautiful Creatures* with Margaret Stohl and for her solo paranormal and contemporary novels. Trained as an educator with a master’s degree in education, she worked for seventeen years as a teacher and reading specialist before turning to writing, a background that shaped her interest in stories for teens and her ongoing involvement with schools, libraries, and literacy programs. She later expanded into graphic novels and comics, writing acclaimed titles for DC such as the *Teen Titans* graphic novel series and *Joker/Harley: Criminal Sanity*, and her work in horror and dark fantasy has earned multiple Bram Stoker Award nominations, including for *Unbreakable*, the first book in The Legion series that includes *Unbreakable (The Legion, Num. 1)*.

Garcia’s novels have been translated into dozens of languages, sold millions of copies worldwide, and reached #1 on the *New York Times* and *USA Today* bestseller lists, securing her a prominent place in contemporary young adult literature. Beyond her writing, she is a cofounder and board member of the YALLFest and YALLWEST book festivals, helping to create major annual gatherings for young readers and authors in Charleston and Santa Monica. She lives in Maryland with her family and their dogs, maintaining close ties to the teen and comics communities through festival work, online engagement, and mentorship of emerging writers.
