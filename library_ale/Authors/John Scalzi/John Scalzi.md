## Biography
John Michael Scalzi II (born 1969) is an American science fiction author whose work combines accessible prose, sharp humor, and social commentary. Raised in a working-class single-parent household in California, he grew up immersed in science fiction and mystery, experiences that later informed both the themes and empathy in his writing. After studying philosophy at the University of Chicago and working as a film critic and columnist, he moved into online writing at AOL before ultimately establishing himself as a full‑time novelist and freelance writer.

Scalzi’s international reputation rests largely on his military science fiction series that begins with *Old Man’s War* and continues through novels such as *The Ghost Brigades*, *The Last Colony*, and *Zoe’s Tale*, which examine aging, identity, colonialism, and the ethics of war through fast‑paced, character‑driven stories. Beyond this series he has produced standalones, space operas, and satirical works, and his long‑running blog “Whatever” has been influential in science fiction fandom and online culture, earning multiple Hugo Awards for fan and related writing. He also served as president of the Science Fiction and Fantasy Writers of America, advocating for authors’ rights and professional standards within the genre community.

In his personal life, Scalzi is known for forthright political and social views, including outspoken support for feminism and LGBTQ rights, which have shaped both his public persona and aspects of his fiction. He lives in Ohio with his wife, Kristine, and their daughter, maintaining close ties to readers through regular blogging, social media, and public appearances at conventions and literary events.
