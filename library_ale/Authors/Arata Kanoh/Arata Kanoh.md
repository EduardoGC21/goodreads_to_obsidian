## Biography
Arata Kanoh is a contemporary Japanese novelist from Aichi Prefecture, known primarily for his close collaboration with filmmaker Makoto Shinkai. After graduating from Aichi Prefectural University, he built a career writing prose adaptations of animated films, translating Shinkai’s visually rich, emotionally nuanced stories into novels that explore characters’ inner lives in greater depth. His work on titles such as "5 Centimeters per Second: one more side" reflects an emphasis on quiet, introspective drama and the emotional distances between people.

Across multiple projects, Kanoh has become one of the key figures responsible for expanding Shinkai’s film worlds into print, including novelizations tied to works like "The Place Promised in Our Early Days" and other major films. In addition to his novels, he served as a screenplay assistant on the internationally acclaimed anime film "Your Name", a role that highlights his integration into the creative teams behind some of the most influential anime productions of the 21st century. Through these collaborations, Kanoh has contributed to shaping how audiences experience these stories across different media, strengthening the bridge between anime cinema and contemporary Japanese light novels.

By consistently focusing on character psychology and the textures of everyday life, Kanoh’s adaptations offer alternative perspectives on well-known narratives, often expanding side characters or unseen moments, as with "5 Centimeters per Second: one more side". This approach has made his work particularly valued by readers who wish to revisit beloved films in a more reflective, literary form, and has secured his place as a notable adaptor and storyteller within modern Japanese popular culture.
