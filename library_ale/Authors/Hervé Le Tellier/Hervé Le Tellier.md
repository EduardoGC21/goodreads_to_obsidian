## Biography
Hervé Le Tellier (born 1957) is a French writer, linguist, and journalist, best known as a leading member and since 2019 the president of Oulipo, the experimental literary group devoted to constrained writing. Born in Paris, he initially trained in mathematics and later became a scientific journalist before completing a doctorate in linguistics, grounding his literary experiments in a strong formal and theoretical background. Over the years he has written novels, short stories, poetry, plays, essays, and many brief, often humorous texts that explore the possibilities and limits of language.

Le Tellier gained wider recognition in France in the late 1990s with works that combined formal constraints with wit and emotional insight. His international breakthrough came with "L’anomalie" ("La anomalía"), which won the Prix Goncourt in 2020 and brought Oulipo-influenced literature to a broad contemporary readership. Alongside his books he has been active in French cultural life as a radio contributor, literary columnist, and editor, and he helped found the mock-philosophical "Association of Friends of Jean-Baptiste Botul," a playful commentary on intellectual life. His career situates him as a central figure in late 20th- and early 21st-century French experimental fiction, bridging rigorous formal play with accessible storytelling.
