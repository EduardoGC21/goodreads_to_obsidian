## Biography
Jeff VanderMeer (born 1968) is an American writer, editor, and critic whose work has been central to the contemporary “New Weird” movement and to environmentally focused speculative fiction. Born in Bellefonte, Pennsylvania, he spent much of his childhood in the Fiji Islands, where his parents worked for the Peace Corps, before returning to the United States and living in New York and Florida. He studied at the University of Florida and in 1992 attended the Clarion Writers Workshop, a formative experience that helped launch his professional writing career.

Beginning in the late 1980s, VanderMeer became a prolific presence in small-press magazines, publishing poetry and fantastical short fiction and running his own imprint, Ministry of Whimsy Press. He gained wider recognition with the Ambergris cycle, including the collection "City of Saints and Madmen," and later achieved international prominence with the Southern Reach trilogy, whose first volume, "Annihilation," won major awards and was adapted into a feature film. His work is noted for its dense, lyrical prose, genre-blurring surrealism, and deep engagement with themes of ecology, transformation, and the uncanny.

VanderMeer has also been influential as an editor, anthologist, and teacher. Alongside his wife, editor Ann VanderMeer, he has co-edited acclaimed anthologies such as "The New Weird" and "The Weird," helping define and showcase innovative fantasy and horror. He has taught at writing workshops, co-founded the Shared Worlds teen writing camp, and written craft and career guides like "Wonderbook" and "Booklife." Now based in Florida, he continues to write fiction and nonfiction that link storytelling to environmental concerns, and he is regarded as one of the most significant voices in contemporary literary speculative fiction.
