## Biography
Martha Wells (born 1964 in Fort Worth, Texas) is an American writer of speculative fiction whose work spans fantasy, science fiction, young adult novels, media tie-ins, and short fiction. Educated in anthropology at Texas A&M University, she became active in the local science fiction and fantasy fandom and honed her craft at workshops such as the Turkey City Writer’s Workshop before publishing her debut novel, “The Element of Fire,” in 1993. She later settled in College Station, Texas, where she has lived with her husband while building a long-term career as a full-time writer.

Wells is especially known for her intricate worldbuilding and the complex, often marginalized characters who inhabit her invented societies, a strength she attributes to her anthropological background. Over the decades she has created multiple notable series, including the Ile-Rien fantasy books and The Books of the Raksura, but her international breakthrough came with the science fiction sequence The Murderbot Diaries, beginning with the novella “All Systems Red.” That work and its sequels have earned her multiple Hugo, Nebula, Locus, Alex, and Dragon Awards, placement on major bestseller lists, and translation into numerous languages, establishing her as one of the leading contemporary voices in genre fiction.

Beyond her fiction, Wells has contributed to major franchises such as Star Wars, Stargate: Atlantis, and Magic: The Gathering, and has been active in the broader SF/F community as a convention guest, workshop instructor, and toastmaster, notably delivering her widely discussed “Unbury the Future” speech on the erasure of marginalized creators. She has been inducted into the Texas Literary Hall of Fame and, more recently, has also served as a consulting producer on the television adaptation of The Murderbot Diaries, further extending her influence from the page to the screen.
