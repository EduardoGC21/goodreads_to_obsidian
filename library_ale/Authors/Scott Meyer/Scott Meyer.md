## Biography
Scott Oscar Meyer (born 1971) is an American author, cartoonist, and humorist best known for blending comedy with speculative fiction. Raised in Sunnyside, Washington, he worked a wide range of jobs—including radio DJ, stand‑up comic, video‑game writer, office manager, and even a “pretend ghost bellhop”—before finding a wider audience through his creative work. His long‑running webcomic “Basic Instructions,” which offered deadpan, instructional humor about everyday life and relationships, first established his voice and brought him a devoted online following.

Building on that success, Meyer turned to prose with his comic fantasy series “Magic 2.0,” beginning with “Off to Be the Wizard” and followed by “Spell or High Water” and “An Unwelcome Quest.” These books, along with stand‑alone novels such as the science‑fictional “Master of Formalities,” showcase his characteristic mix of genre playfulness, character‑driven comedy, and interest in technology and social dynamics. His work has resonated especially with readers who enjoy self‑aware, reference‑rich storytelling.

Meyer’s personal life and creative output are closely intertwined: his comics have drawn on his relationships and day‑to‑day experiences, and he often writes about ordinary people dropped into extraordinary, absurd situations. He lives in the United States with his wife, and has at various times been based in places such as Florida and Arizona, continuing to write humorous genre fiction and occasionally returning to his roots in cartooning.
