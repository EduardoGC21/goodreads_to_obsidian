## Biography
Kai-Fu Lee (born 1961) is a Taiwanese computer scientist, technology executive, investor, and author whose career has bridged leading academic research in artificial intelligence and senior roles at major U.S. technology companies. Raised in Taipei and later in the United States, he studied computer science at Columbia University and earned a PhD from Carnegie Mellon University, where his work on the SPHINX speech recognition system and related research helped establish him as an early pioneer in machine learning and speech technologies. After a brief period in academia, he moved into industry, holding influential positions at Apple and Silicon Graphics before becoming the founding director of Microsoft Research China (later Microsoft Research Asia), a lab that trained a generation of Chinese AI researchers and became one of the world’s most respected computer science research centers.

Lee later served as a corporate vice president at Microsoft and then as president of Google China, becoming a central figure in the expansion of U.S. internet companies into the Chinese market. His high-profile move from Microsoft to Google led to a widely watched legal dispute over non-compete agreements, underscoring his strategic importance in the global tech industry. In 2009, he left Google to found the venture capital firm Innovation Works, later Sinovation Ventures, through which he has funded and mentored numerous Chinese technology startups, further cementing his role as a bridge between Silicon Valley and China’s innovation ecosystem.

As an author and public intellectual, Lee has used books such as “AI Superpowers: China, Silicon Valley, and the New World Order” to explain the rise of Chinese AI, the dynamics of data-driven innovation, and the societal impacts of automation. He has been open about his experience with serious illness, using it to reflect on work, meaning, and human values in an age of rapid technological change. Through his writing, media appearances, and leadership roles, he has become one of the most internationally recognized voices on artificial intelligence and its implications for the global balance of technological power.
