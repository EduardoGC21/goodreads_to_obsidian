## Biography
Blake Crouch (born 1978) is an American novelist and screenwriter known for blending science fiction, thriller, and horror into fast‑paced, emotionally driven stories. Raised near Statesville, North Carolina, he studied English and creative writing at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, graduating in 2000, and began publishing fiction soon after with early novels like *Desert Places* and *Locked Doors*. Those works established his interest in morally conflicted characters and ordinary people trapped in extreme situations.

Crouch’s breakthrough came with the dystopian Wayward Pines trilogy, which was adapted into a FOX television series, bringing his work to a global audience and leading to further work in film and television. He went on to write high‑concept science‑fiction thrillers such as *Dark Matter*, *Recursion*, and *Upgrade*, which explore identity, memory, time, and the malleability of reality; *Dark Matter* in particular became a major bestseller and was adapted as a television series, with Crouch serving in key creative roles. Across novels and screen projects, he has become a prominent contemporary figure in speculative fiction, known for combining accessible science with suspense and intimate family stakes.

Crouch has lived for many years in Colorado, balancing an active writing career with work as a producer and showrunner on adaptations of his books. His stories often reflect his own preoccupations with choice, consequence, and alternate possibilities in ordinary lives, and his commercial success has helped make science‑fiction thrillers more visible in mainstream popular culture.
