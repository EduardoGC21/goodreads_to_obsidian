## Biography
Barbara Truelove is an Australian author and game designer whose work blends speculative science fiction, horror, and queer sensibilities. Before breaking out in traditional publishing, she built a career in game design and interactive fiction, channeling her fascination with monsters, technology, and identity into narrative-driven projects.

Her interactive novel "Blood Moon" (2023) first brought her wider attention, but it was the sci‑fi horror novel "Of Monsters and Mainframes" (2025) that established her as a significant new voice in genre fiction, earning major bestseller status and multiple award recognitions. Across her work, Truelove tends to center characters who see themselves as outsiders, using found-family dynamics, dark humor, and pulpy genre tropes to explore what it means to be considered a "monster" by society.

Away from the page and the screen, Truelove is known for her enthusiasm for the strange and the tactile, reportedly keeping a growing collection of rocks and other curios. Her dual background in games and prose writing informs her interest in immersive, character-focused storytelling, making her a notable figure at the intersection of contemporary speculative fiction and narrative game design.
