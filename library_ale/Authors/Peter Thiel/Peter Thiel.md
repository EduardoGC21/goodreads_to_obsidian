## Biography
Peter Thiel (b. 1967) is a German-American entrepreneur, investor, and political activist best known as a co-founder of PayPal and an early backer of Facebook. Born in Frankfurt, West Germany, he moved with his family to the United States as a child and grew up in California. Thiel studied philosophy at Stanford University and later earned a law degree there, becoming involved with libertarian and conservative student politics, including co-founding the newspaper The Stanford Review. After a brief stint in corporate law and derivatives trading, he turned to entrepreneurship and venture capital.

In 1998 Thiel co-founded the online payments company PayPal, serving as its CEO and helping to guide it through rapid growth and its eventual sale to eBay in 2002. He then founded the hedge fund Clarium Capital and became a highly influential venture capitalist in Silicon Valley, most notably as the first outside investor in Facebook. Thiel later co-founded data analytics firm Palantir Technologies, which became a major contractor for government and intelligence agencies. His book “Zero to One” (originally based on a course he taught at Stanford and published in Spanish as “De cero a uno: Cómo inventar el futuro”) articulated his views on innovation, monopoly, and startup strategy, shaping entrepreneurial thinking worldwide.

Thiel has also been a prominent and sometimes polarizing public figure because of his political and cultural interventions. He has funded a range of libertarian and conservative causes, supported outsider political candidates, and has been involved in high-profile legal disputes, including financing litigation that contributed to the shutdown of the media outlet Gawker. His career combines entrepreneurial success, contrarian intellectual positions, and deep involvement in the intersection of technology, finance, and politics.
