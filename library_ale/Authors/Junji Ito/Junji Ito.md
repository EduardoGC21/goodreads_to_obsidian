## Biography
Junji Ito (born 1963) is a Japanese horror manga artist renowned for his distinctive blend of psychological dread, body horror, and quietly escalating unease. Raised in Gifu Prefecture, he developed an early fascination with the macabre while reading horror manga and stories, particularly influenced by Kazuo Umezu and H. P. Lovecraft. Trained as a dental technician, he initially created manga as a hobby before winning an honorable mention in a manga award contest, which led to his professional debut and a rapid rise as one of Japan’s most original voices in horror.

Ito’s work is marked by meticulous linework, eerie calm settings disrupted by the grotesque, and characters whose ordinary lives are gradually consumed by irrational forces. Over his career he has produced numerous short stories and longer serializations that explore themes of obsession, transformation, and the fragility of sanity. His adaptations, such as his manga version of Osamu Dazai’s "No Longer Human," show his ability to reinterpret classic literature through a horror lens, emphasizing alienation and inner torment. International recognition, critical acclaim, and multiple awards have cemented his status as a leading figure in contemporary horror comics, influencing artists and creators worldwide.

Known for being soft-spoken and reflective in interviews, Ito has maintained a relatively private personal life, though his affection for cats, everyday oddities, and dark humor sometimes appears in his lighter works. Despite the disturbing imagery of his stories, he is frequently described as gentle and modest, a contrast that underscores the imaginative leap between his daily life and the nightmarish worlds he creates.
