## Biography
Peter Attia (born 1973) is a Canadian‑American physician, author, and longevity specialist whose work has helped popularize a data‑driven, preventive approach to health and aging. Raised in Toronto by Egyptian Christian immigrant parents, he studied mechanical engineering and applied mathematics at Queen’s University before earning his medical degree from Stanford University School of Medicine. He trained in general surgery at Johns Hopkins Hospital and conducted research at the National Cancer Institute on immune‑based therapies for cancer, experiences that grounded his later interest in chronic disease prevention and metabolic health.

After leaving surgical training, Attia worked as a consultant at McKinsey & Company before founding a medical practice focused on “Medicine 3.0,” emphasizing early detection of risk, rigorous lifestyle interventions, and individualized longevity strategies. He gained a wide audience through his writing, including the bestseller “Outlive: The Science & Art of Longevity,” and through his podcast The Peter Attia Drive, where he interviews scientists, clinicians, and thinkers on topics related to lifespan and healthspan. His public profile has made him an influential, and sometimes controversial, figure in modern preventive and longevity medicine, as he navigates the tension between cutting‑edge science, popular health advice, and scrutiny of his professional and personal associations.

Attia has been recognized for shaping public discourse around aging and health optimization, and he remains an active commentator on nutrition, exercise, sleep, and emotional well‑being as pillars of long‑term health. He lives in the United States with his wife and three children, and continues to practice, write, and speak about how individuals can extend not only how long they live but how well they live over time.
