## Biography
Becky Chambers (b. 1985) is an American science fiction author best known for character-driven, optimistic space opera and contemplative speculative fiction. Raised in California in a family connected to both space science and the performing arts, she developed an early fascination with astronomy and storytelling that would later shape her fiction. Before becoming a full‑time writer, Chambers held various jobs including theater work and freelance writing, experiences that informed her nuanced portrayals of community, work, and belonging.

Chambers first gained wide attention with "The Long Way to a Small, Angry Planet," the opening novel in her Wayfarers series, which she initially crowdfunded before it was picked up by a traditional publisher. The book and its sequels cemented her reputation for compassionate, inclusive narratives that foreground everyday lives, found family, and cross‑cultural understanding in imaginative futures. She continued to explore philosophical, hopeful science fiction in works such as the "Monk & Robot" novellas, which examine purpose, labor, and coexistence in a gentle, post‑industrial world.

Celebrated for her humane approach to speculative storytelling, Chambers has received major genre honors, including the Hugo Award for Best Series for Wayfarers. Her work is often cited as central to the emergence of "hopepunk" and related movements emphasizing kindness, mutual aid, and ethical complexity in genre fiction. She lives with her wife and has become an influential voice for inclusive, character‑focused science fiction that imagines better futures without ignoring moral and emotional challenges.
