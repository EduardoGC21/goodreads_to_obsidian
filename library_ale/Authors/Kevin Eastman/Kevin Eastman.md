## Biography
Kevin Brooks Eastman (born 1962) is an American comic book artist, writer, and publisher best known as the co-creator of the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles with Peter Laird. Raised in Portland, Maine, he was an avid comics reader inspired by Jack Kirby and other visionary creators. After moving to Northampton, Massachusetts in the early 1980s, he partnered with Laird, and in 1984 they self-published the first black-and-white issue of Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles under the Mirage Studios banner, turning a late-night joke sketch into a global multimedia phenomenon spanning comics, animation, film, toys, and games.

On the strength of that success, Eastman became an important advocate for creator rights and independent comics. He founded Tundra Publishing in 1990 to help cartoonists retain ownership of their work and later purchased and ran the influential science-fiction and fantasy comics magazine Heavy Metal, further championing adult-oriented and experimental graphic storytelling. In addition to returning periodically to the Turtles—including co-writing projects like “Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: The Last Ronin”—he has created and produced other series and graphic novels, often collaborating with a wide network of artists and writers.

Eastman’s personal life has intersected with his creative world; he has been married more than once, including to actress Julie Strain in the 1990s, and has at least one son. He has also worked as a producer, occasional actor, and art collector, founding the Words & Pictures Museum dedicated to comic and cartoon art. His career is widely regarded as emblematic of the rise of creator-owned comics and the mainstreaming of independent graphic storytelling.
