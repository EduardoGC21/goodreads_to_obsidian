## Biography
Sequoia Nagamatsu is a Japanese American writer whose work blends literary fiction with speculative and science-fiction elements to explore grief, climate change, and the bonds between people across time. Raised in Northern California and Hawaii, he has often drawn on his bicultural background and a sense of displacement to craft stories that move between myth, futurism, and intimate family drama. His acclaimed novel "How High We Go in the Dark" follows interconnected characters living through a climate plague, showcasing his interest in how individuals and communities respond to catastrophe with resilience and tenderness.

Before gaining wider recognition as a novelist, Nagamatsu published the story collection "Where We Go When All We Were Is Gone," which reimagines Japanese folklore and popular culture, further cementing his reputation for genre-blurring narratives. In addition to his books, he has taught creative writing and contributed fiction to numerous literary journals, mentoring younger writers and participating in contemporary conversations about Asian American identity, speculative literature, and the ethics of imagining future worlds. He continues to write and teach in the United States, where his work is regarded as a significant contribution to 21st-century speculative and literary fiction.
