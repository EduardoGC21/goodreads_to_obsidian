## Biography
Andrea Chapela (born 1990) is a Mexican writer whose work bridges fantasy, science fiction, and essay, often exploring perception, technology, and intimate relationships. Raised in Mexico City, she developed an early vocation for storytelling, beginning to write as a child and publishing the first volume of her young adult fantasy tetralogy Vâudïz while still a teenager. Alongside her literary interests she studied chemistry at the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM), a scientific background that later infused her fiction and essays with a distinctive attention to scientific metaphors and speculative ideas.

Chapela continued her formation abroad, earning an MFA in Creative Writing in Spanish at the University of Iowa and later pursuing graduate studies in Asian and African (Japan) studies at El Colegio de México. She has been awarded multiple prestigious Mexican national prizes, including the Gilberto Owen National Prize for Literature, the Juan José Arreola National Prize for Short Story, and the José Luis Martínez National Prize for Young Essayists, for books such as the story collection "Ansibles, perfiladores y otras máquinas de ingenio" and the essay collection "Grados de miopía." Her work, which also includes the story collection "Un año de servicio a la habitación" and the novel "Todos los fines del mundo," has been translated into several languages, and in 2021 she was recognized by Granta as one of the best young Spanish‑language novelists.

Beyond her books, Chapela has been an active participant in contemporary Hispanic letters as a teacher, translator, and cultural organizer. She has held scholarships from Mexico’s FONCA program and from the Ayuntamiento de Madrid’s Residencia de Estudiantes, experiences that fed both her international outlook and the settings of her fiction. She has taught Spanish in the United States, contributed essays and stories to numerous magazines, and taken part in genre‑focused projects such as the Mexicona: Imaginación y Futuro gatherings, all while continuing to live and write in Mexico City, where she shares her life with her two cats.
