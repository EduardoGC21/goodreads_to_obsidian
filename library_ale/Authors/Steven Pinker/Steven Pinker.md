## Biography
Steven Pinker (born 1954) is a Canadian-American cognitive psychologist, linguist, and popular science writer known for his work on language, the mind, and the broader trajectory of human progress. Born and raised in Montreal, Quebec, he studied at McGill University and later earned his PhD from Harvard, going on to hold academic posts at institutions such as MIT and Harvard. His research and teaching have focused on visual cognition, language acquisition, and psycholinguistics, helping to shape contemporary understandings of how humans perceive, think, and communicate.

Beyond his academic contributions, Pinker has become widely known through his accessible books and essays, in which he argues that reason, science, and humanism have led to long-term improvements in human welfare. In works like "Enlightenment Now: The Case for Reason, Science, Humanism, and Progress," he synthesizes data from history, economics, and public health to contend that violence has declined and living standards have risen over time. His writing and public engagement have made him a prominent, sometimes controversial, voice in debates about human nature, morality, politics, and the role of Enlightenment values in modern societies.
