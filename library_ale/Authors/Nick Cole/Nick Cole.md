## Biography
Nick Cole is an American science fiction and fantasy author whose work blends military experience, post-apocalyptic settings, and cinematic action. A former U.S. Army soldier and working actor, he draws heavily on his time in uniform and in front of the camera to give his stories a gritty realism and visual flair. Living in Southern California, he has balanced auditions for commercials, sitcom roles, and student films with a steady output of genre fiction.

Cole first came to wide attention with his debut novel "The Old Man and the Wasteland," which became an Amazon #1 bestseller in Science Fiction and established him as a distinctive voice in post-apocalyptic storytelling. He later gained further prominence with "CTRL ALT Revolt!," an apocalyptic thriller that won the 2016 Dragon Award for Best Apocalyptic Novel, underscoring his appeal among contemporary science fiction readers. His collaboration with Jason Anspach on the "Galaxy’s Edge" series has produced a bestselling, award‑winning military science fiction saga, noted for its action, humor, and vivid depiction of soldierly camaraderie.

Beyond awards and sales, Cole’s significance lies in the way he channels his lived experience—both as a soldier and as an actor—into accessible, fast‑paced narratives that resonate with fans of modern military and apocalyptic science fiction. He continues to write and collaborate from Southern California, contributing to a shared-universe style of storytelling that has helped build an engaged readership around his work.
