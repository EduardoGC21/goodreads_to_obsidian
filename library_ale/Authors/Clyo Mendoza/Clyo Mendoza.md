## Biography
Clyo Mendoza (born 1993 in Oaxaca, Mexico) is a Mexican poet and novelist whose work explores violence, desire, memory, and the lives of people in marginalized and desolate landscapes. Trained in Hispanic Literature at the Universidad Autónoma Metropolitana, she first gained recognition with the poetry collections *Anamnesis* (2016) and *Silencio* (2018), the latter awarded the Premio Internacional de Poesía Sor Juana Inés de la Cruz, making her one of the most prominent younger voices in contemporary Mexican letters. Her writing often draws on oral storytelling, feminist and gender‑violence perspectives, and the experiences of Indigenous and rural communities.

Mendoza has been awarded scholarships from Mexico’s Fondo Nacional para la Cultura y las Artes in both poetry and novel categories, and she has held residencies at the Fundación Antonio Gala in Córdoba, Spain, among others. Her debut novel *Furia* (2021) extends the concerns of her poetry into a stark narrative of war‑torn territories and fractured families, and its critical success—including prizes such as the Premio Javier Morote and Amazon’s Premio Primera Novela—helped bring her work to international attention through translations into several languages. Alongside literature, she has participated in transdisciplinary projects involving painting, photography, theatre, and sound collage, collaborating with artists and collectives to respond to Mexico’s social and gendered violence while maintaining a strong commitment to experimental, hybrid forms of storytelling.

She continues to live and work in Oaxaca, balancing her literary career with artistic collaborations and personal life, and is regarded as one of the leading figures of a new generation of Mexican writers who confront contemporary realities—especially femicide and disappearances—through formally innovative and emotionally intense narratives.
