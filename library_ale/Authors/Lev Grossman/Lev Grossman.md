## Biography
Lev Grossman (b. 1969) is an American novelist, journalist, and critic best known for his fantasy series that begins with "The Magicians." Born in Concord, Massachusetts, he grew up in an intellectually engaged family and studied literature at Harvard University before pursuing graduate work at Yale. His early career included work as a technology and book critic, which sharpened his sensitivity to both classical literature and contemporary pop culture.

Grossman gained international recognition with "The Magicians," which reimagines portal and school-of-magic fantasies in a darker, more psychologically realistic key, following disaffected, highly intelligent young adults as they grapple with power, trauma, and meaning. The trilogy helped push fantasy further into mainstream literary conversation, appealing to both genre and literary audiences. Alongside his fiction, Grossman spent many years as a book critic and technology writer for major American media outlets, where he championed genre fiction and helped elevate discussions around contemporary fantasy and science fiction.

His work often explores themes of escapism, depression, and the transition from adolescence to adulthood, reflecting both personal and generational concerns. Grossman has been active in literary and fan communities, appearing at conventions and festivals, mentoring emerging writers, and contributing essays about the value of imaginative literature in modern life.
