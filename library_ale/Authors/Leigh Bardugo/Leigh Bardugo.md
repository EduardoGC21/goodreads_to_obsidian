## Biography
Leigh Bardugo (born 1975) is a fantasy author whose work bridges young adult and adult fiction, best known for creating the Grishaverse beginning with the novel “Shadow and Bone” and for her darker campus-fantasy series that includes “Ninth House” and its sequel “Hell Bent.” Born in Jerusalem and raised in Los Angeles by her grandparents, she grew up in a secular Jewish family with Sephardic, Russian, and Lithuanian roots, experiences that later informed the cultural textures and themes of power, faith, and marginalization in her fiction.

Bardugo studied English at Yale University, graduating in 1997, and before turning to novels she worked in copywriting, journalism, and in makeup and special effects. Her breakout success with the Grishaverse led to international recognition, numerous award nominations and wins, and screen adaptations, including the Netflix series “Shadow and Bone,” where she also served as an executive producer. With her Alex Stern novels, including “Hell Bent,” she moved into acclaimed adult fantasy, using magic and secret societies as vehicles to explore trauma, privilege, and resilience.

Living in the United States, Bardugo has spoken openly about living with osteonecrosis and sometimes using a cane, a reality she wove into the character of Kaz Brekker in her Six of Crows duology. She has also been active within the writing community, lending her voice to conversations about representation, disability, and the ethics of emerging technologies in publishing, further cementing her influence beyond the page.
