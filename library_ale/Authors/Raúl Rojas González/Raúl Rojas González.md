## Biography
Raúl Rojas González (born 1955) is a Mexican-born computer scientist, mathematician, and historian of science whose work has bridged technical innovation and the public understanding of mathematics and computing. Raised and educated initially in Mexico, he later continued his academic career in Germany, becoming a professor at the Free University of Berlin. His research has spanned artificial intelligence, neural networks, and the history of computing, and he has been involved in projects such as autonomous vehicles and the reconstruction and analysis of early computers, helping to illuminate how modern information technology emerged.

Beyond his technical research, Rojas has become well known as an explainer of mathematical ideas to wider audiences. In works such as “El lenguaje de las matemáticas. Historias de sus símbolos,” he explores how mathematical notation and symbols developed historically, connecting abstract concepts to cultural and scientific contexts. Throughout his career, he has been recognized for both his scholarly contributions and his efforts to make mathematics and computer science more accessible, positioning him as an influential figure in contemporary Hispanic and European academic life.
