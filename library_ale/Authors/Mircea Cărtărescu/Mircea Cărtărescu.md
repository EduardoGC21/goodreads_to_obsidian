## Biography
Mircea Cărtărescu (1956–) is a Romanian novelist, poet, essayist, and literary critic whose work has become central to contemporary European literature. Born in Bucharest in 1956, he grew up under the Ceaușescu dictatorship and was shaped by both the restrictive cultural climate of communist Romania and the liberating influence of 1960s Western counterculture. He studied Romanian language and literature at the University of Bucharest, where he later earned a doctorate with a thesis on Romanian postmodernism and began a parallel career as a literary scholar and university professor.

Cărtărescu first gained recognition as a poet in the late 1970s and 1980s, but it is with his ambitious, visionary prose—most notably the “Orbitor/Blinding” trilogy and the collection later known as “Nostalgia,” which includes the roulette‑obsessed tale often translated as “The Roulette Player” or “El ruletista”—that he established himself as one of Romania’s most important postmodern writers. His books, dense with memory, dream imagery, and metafictional play, turn Bucharest and his own biography into expansive literary universes, and they have been widely translated, bringing unprecedented international visibility to Romanian literature. Over the decades he has received numerous national and international prizes and has often been mentioned among candidates for major global literary awards.

Alongside his creative work, Cărtărescu has been an influential critic and public intellectual, teaching at the University of Bucharest and serving as a prominent voice in post-1989 Romanian cultural debates. His marriage to fellow Romanian writer Ioana Nicolaie places him within a vibrant literary milieu, and his essays and columns have helped shape discussions about the legacy of communism, the role of literature, and Romania’s place within Europe. His blend of autobiographical reflection, philosophical ambition, and formal experimentation has made him a key figure in understanding the evolution of Eastern European literature after the fall of the Iron Curtain.
