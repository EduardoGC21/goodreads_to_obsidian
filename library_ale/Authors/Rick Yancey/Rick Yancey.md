## Biography
Rick Yancey (born 1962) is an American author best known for his work in young adult science fiction and fantasy. Before becoming a full-time writer, he held various jobs, including working for the U.S. Internal Revenue Service, experiences that preceded his eventual focus on fiction writing. He began publishing in the early 2000s, initially gaining attention for his adult and young adult novels that often blend suspense, speculative elements, and character-driven storytelling.

Yancey achieved international recognition with his post-apocalyptic young adult series that begins with "The 5th Wave," in which he explores survival, trust, and humanity under the pressure of an alien invasion. The subsequent book "The Infinite Sea" continued to expand his readership and solidified his reputation as a major figure in contemporary YA literature. Over his career, he has written across age categories and genres, and his work has been translated into multiple languages and adapted for film, extending his influence beyond the literary world.

Known for his introspective characters and morally complex plots, Yancey has become a significant voice in speculative fiction for younger readers. His stories often examine how ordinary people respond to extraordinary crises, setting personal relationships and ethical choices against large-scale existential threats. He continues to write and publish, maintaining an active presence in the field of young adult and speculative fiction.
