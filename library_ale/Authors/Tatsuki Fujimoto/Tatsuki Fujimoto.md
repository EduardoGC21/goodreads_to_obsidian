## Biography
Tatsuki Fujimoto is a Japanese manga artist best known internationally for the dark fantasy series "Fire Punch" and "Chainsaw Man." Born in the rural city of Nikaho in Akita Prefecture, he grew up surrounded by nature and began drawing at a young age. With no nearby preparatory art schools, he joined oil‑painting classes attended by his grandparents and went on to study Western painting at the Tohoku University of Art and Design, where he refined the painterly sensibility that later informed his manga’s cinematic compositions and striking imagery.

Fujimoto’s professional career started in his late teens, when he submitted early one‑shot works to Shueisha contests, earning jury prizes and honorable mentions that led to his magazine debut. His breakthrough came with the digital serialization of "Fire Punch" (2016–2018) on Shōnen Jump+, followed by "Chainsaw Man" beginning in 2018, whose mix of violence, dark humor, and emotional vulnerability made him one of the most talked‑about new mangaka of his generation. "Chainsaw Man, Vol. 1: Dog and Chainsaw" introduced many readers worldwide to his distinctive approach to pacing, character psychology, and genre subversion.

Fujimoto’s work has received major industry recognition, including the Shogakukan Manga Award for Best Shōnen Manga and multiple Harvey Awards for Best Manga. He continues to collaborate closely with editor Shihei Lin while also experimenting with acclaimed one‑shots such as "Look Back" and "Goodbye, Eri," solidifying his reputation as a creator who combines commercial success with formally adventurous storytelling.
