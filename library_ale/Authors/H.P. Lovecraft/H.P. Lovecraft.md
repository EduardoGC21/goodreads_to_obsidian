## Biography
Howard Phillips Lovecraft (1890–1937) was an American writer of weird fiction whose work profoundly reshaped twentieth-century horror and science fiction. Born in Providence, Rhode Island, he spent most of his life in New England, an environment whose architecture, history, and decaying grandeur deeply influenced his imagination. His childhood was marked by family instability and illness—his father suffered a mental breakdown and died in an institution, and Lovecraft himself struggled with fragile health, anxiety, and periods of isolation that fueled both his self-education and his pessimistic worldview.

Lovecraft is best known as the creator of the so‑called Cthulhu Mythos, a loose fictional universe centered on ancient cosmic entities and the insignificance of humanity in a vast, indifferent cosmos. Stories like "At the Mountains of Madness" exemplify his blend of antiquarian interests, cosmic horror, and pseudo‑scholarly detail, often told through diaries, letters, or reports that give his fiction a sense of documentary realism. Though he published mainly in pulp magazines such as Weird Tales and never achieved financial success in his lifetime, he corresponded extensively with other writers, mentored younger authors, and encouraged them to borrow and expand his ideas, making his work highly collaborative in spirit.

Lovecraft’s attitudes were shaped by his class consciousness, antiquarianism, and strong regional identity, but also marred by pronounced racism and xenophobia, which appear in both his letters and some of his fiction and have provoked substantial critical reassessment. He married Sonia H. Greene and briefly lived in New York City, but the marriage failed and he returned to Providence, where he continued to write until his death from intestinal cancer and related complications in 1937. Posthumously, his reputation grew through the efforts of friends and admirers who preserved and reprinted his stories, securing his status as a central figure in modern horror literature despite the troubling aspects of his personal beliefs.
