## Biography
Caroline Hardaker (b. 1986) is an English poet, novelist, and librettist from the north east of England, whose work blends speculative themes with lyrical, often unsettling prose. Educated at Newcastle University, where she later worked in research communications, she has built a career that straddles poetry, fiction, and opera, frequently exploring folklore, climate anxiety, the uncanny, and the fragile boundaries between human beings and their environments. Before turning to longer fiction, she published poetry collections such as "Bone Ovation" and "Little Quakes Every Day," gaining attention for pieces that meditate on time, memory, and the body.

Hardaker’s debut novel, "Composite Creatures," marked her entry into science fiction and horror with a near-future narrative about environmental collapse, corporate medicine, and the ethics of survival, and it was recognized with accolades including a shortlist spot for the Kitschies’ Golden Tentacle Award. She followed this with "Mothtown," a genre-blurring work of fantasy and horror set in northern England that examines grief, mental health, and the lure of hidden worlds. Alongside her books, Hardaker remains active in the broader arts, serving as writer-in-residence for events such as the Moving Parts Newcastle Puppetry Festival and collaborating with the Royal Northern College of Music on operatic song cycles, while also appearing on panels and in journals to discuss cross-genre writing, philosophy, and contemporary speculative literature.
