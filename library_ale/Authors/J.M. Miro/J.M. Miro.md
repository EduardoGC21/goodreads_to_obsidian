## Biography
J.M. Miro is the fantasy pseudonym of Canadian poet and novelist Steven Price, who was born and raised in British Columbia and has built a reputation for richly atmospheric, historically rooted fiction. Trained as a poet, with degrees from the University of Victoria and the University of Virginia, he first established himself through award‑winning poetry collections before turning to novels that blend meticulous research with lyrical prose. Writing as J.M. Miro, he created *Ordinary Monsters*, the opening volume of The Talents Trilogy, which transposes his longstanding fascination with the Victorian era into a dark, expansive fantasy about gifted children, power, and belonging.

Beyond his work under the Miro name, Price has emerged as a significant contemporary Canadian voice, known for novels such as *By Gaslight* and *Lampedusa* that explore memory, identity, and the legacies of the past. He teaches poetry and fiction at the University of Victoria, helping to shape younger writers while continuing his own body of work. His life and writing are closely intertwined with that of his spouse, acclaimed Canadian novelist Esi Edugyan; together they form a prominent literary partnership based in Victoria, British Columbia, balancing family life with internationally recognized literary careers.

Across his careers as Steven Price and J.M. Miro, his work is marked by a blend of poetic language, carefully crafted historical settings, and an interest in characters who live at the margins—outsiders, exiles, and the gifted or haunted. *Ordinary Monsters* and its sequels extend these concerns into the realm of fantasy, using supernatural Talents and a gaslit, alternate 19th century to examine questions of difference, found family, and what it means to be monstrous or human. This dual identity allows him to move fluidly between literary and genre traditions while contributing significantly to Canadian and international speculative fiction.
