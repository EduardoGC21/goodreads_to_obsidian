## Biography
Stuart Turton (born 1980) is an English novelist and former journalist whose inventive crime and mystery fiction has reached readers around the world. Raised in Widnes in the north of England, he studied English and Philosophy at the University of Liverpool before spending a year teaching in Shanghai. He later worked as a technology journalist in London and as a travel journalist in Dubai, experiences that broadened his view of different cultures and histories and would later feed into the rich settings of his fiction.

After returning to the UK, Turton devoted himself to writing his debut novel, published in the US as "The 7 1/2 Deaths of Evelyn Hardcastle." The book’s intricate, high‑concept structure and blend of classic detective fiction with speculative elements made it an international bestseller and earned major awards such as the Costa First Novel Award and the Books Are My Bag Readers Award. He has since continued to develop a reputation for ambitious, puzzle‑driven narratives that combine locked‑room mystery traditions with historical, fantastical, and science‑fictional themes.

Turton lives near London with his wife and daughters. Known for his self‑deprecating humor in interviews and author essays, he describes his work as deliberately “nuts,” embracing complex plots and genre fusion while remaining grounded in character and emotional stakes. His success has made him a prominent contemporary figure in British crime and speculative fiction, with his books translated into numerous languages and adapted, or optioned for adaptation, for screen.
