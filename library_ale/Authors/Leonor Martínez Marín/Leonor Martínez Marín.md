## Biography
Leonor Martínez Marín (Leonor Martínez Martín) (1930–2013) was a Spanish arabist, translator, and scholar of Semitic philology, born and educated in Barcelona. After completing secondary school she entered the University of Barcelona, where she studied Philosophy and Letters with a specialization in Semitic Philology, eventually earning both her licentiate and doctorate. She joined the same university as faculty in the 1950s, first as an assistant and later as a tenured professor, and spent decades training new generations of specialists in Arabic language, metrics, and literature. Her rigorous approach combined close textual work with careful historical and cultural contextualization, making her a central figure in the institutional consolidation of Arabic studies in Spain.

Martínez Marín became widely known for her translations and editorial work, which opened Arabic literature to Spanish-speaking audiences. Her "Antología de poesía árabe contemporánea" brought together a broad range of modern Arab poets and remains a landmark in the study and reception of contemporary Arabic poetry in Spanish, while her co‑translation of "Las mil y una noches" and numerous other translations and studies helped establish a serious, philologically grounded tradition of Arabic literary translation in Spain. Married to the prominent arabist Juan Vernet Ginés, with whom she had three daughters, she maintained a distinct scholarly profile of her own and supervised several important doctoral theses, leaving a strong academic legacy reflected in both her students’ work and the dedicated volume "Traducir el mundo árabe. Homenaje a Leonor Martínez" that followed her death.

Her personal library, rich in works on Arabic and Islamic studies as well as on philology and literature more broadly, was donated to the University of Barcelona, ensuring the continuity of her impact on future scholars. Remembered by colleagues and students for her intellectual integrity and pedagogical dedication, Martínez Marín is regarded as one of the key figures in twentieth‑century Spanish arabism, whose work significantly broadened the understanding of the Arab world and its literary traditions in the Spanish cultural sphere.
