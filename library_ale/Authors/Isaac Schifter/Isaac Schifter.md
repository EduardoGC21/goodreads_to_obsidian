## Biography
Isaac Schifter (often cited as Isaac Schifter Secora) is a Mexican chemist and science communicator whose career bridges academic research, industrial innovation and public outreach on environmental and energy issues. Trained as a pharmaceutical chemist-biologist at the Faculty of Chemistry of the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM), he went on to earn a doctorate in chemistry from the Université Claude Bernard Lyon 1 in France. Since the early 1970s he has worked primarily at the Instituto Mexicano del Petróleo (Mexican Institute of Petroleum), where he has held leadership positions in research and technological development, particularly in areas linked to the oil industry, pipeline integrity and the transformation of energy resources.

Schifter’s scientific work focuses on catalysis, materials science and environmental protection, and he has obtained numerous patents arising from research conducted at the Instituto Mexicano del Petróleo. His standing in the Mexican scientific community is reflected in his recognition as an emeritus member of the Sistema Nacional de Investigadores and in distinctions such as the 1995 Andrés Manuel del Río National Chemistry Award for technology development. Beyond his technical contributions, he has written accessible books that explain complex topics such as chaos theory, climate change, water scarcity and fuels to general audiences—for example, in "La ciencia del caos" he introduces the study of chaotic behavior in science to non-specialist readers, while other works address the environmental footprint of water and fossil fuels.

Over several decades, Schifter has collaborated closely with other Mexican scientists and communicators, coauthoring books on climate, energy and the Anthropocene alongside colleagues such as Carmen González-Macías and Manuel Guerrero. Through this combination of high-level research, institutional leadership and clear public communication, he has helped shape contemporary Mexican discussion on the environmental consequences of industrial development and on the technological options for mitigating climate change and pollution.
