## Biography
David Wong is the pen name of American writer Jason Pargin, born in 1975, who became widely known for blending horror, dark comedy, and contemporary social commentary. He first gained attention as a humor columnist and later as executive editor at Cracked.com, where his online essays and serialized fiction developed a strong following. Under the Wong pseudonym, he honed a distinctive voice that combined absurdist scenarios with grounded, often empathetic observations about ordinary people in extraordinary situations.

His debut novel, "John Dies at the End," which began as an online serial, became a cult favorite and was later adapted into a feature film, establishing him as a notable figure in modern horror-comedy. Pargin has continued to publish novels that explore themes of fear, responsibility, and the bizarre undercurrents of everyday life, often focusing on flawed but relatable protagonists. Beyond his fiction, he is recognized for his accessible reflections on anxiety, work, and modern culture, which have contributed to his reputation as both an entertainer and a thoughtful commentator on contemporary society.
