## Biography
Pedro Bosch Giral is a Mexican physicist and science communicator who has dedicated his career to both research and teaching, as well as to bringing scientific ideas to a broad readership. He earned his degree in physics at the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM) and later completed a doctorate at Claude Bernard University in Lyon, France, training in a strongly international academic environment that shaped his approach to science. Professionally he has worked at the Mexican petroleum industry’s research sector, including the Instituto del Petróleo, and he has served as a professor at the Universidad Autónoma Metropolitana, Iztapalapa campus, where he has contributed to educating generations of students in physics and related fields.

Alongside his academic and technical work, Bosch Giral has become known for blending scientific explanation with narrative fiction, using the tools of the novel and the short story to make topics in chemistry and physics accessible and engaging. In collaboration with chemist Graciela Pacheco he wrote "El carbono: cuentos orientales", which uses tales inspired by the world of The Thousand and One Nights to explore the properties and history of carbon, and in other works such as "Fuego en el alma y en la vida infierno" he adopts the structure of crime and detective fiction to illuminate concepts related to heat, fire, and modern technology. His books, published by Fondo de Cultura Económica in the popular "La Ciencia para Todos" collection, situate scientific knowledge within everyday life and literary tradition, making him a significant figure in contemporary Spanish-language popular science writing.
