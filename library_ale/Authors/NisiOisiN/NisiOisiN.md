## Biography
Nisio Isin (often stylized as NISIOISIN) is a contemporary Japanese novelist and manga author best known for his inventive light novel series, including the Monogatari cycle, Zaregoto, and Katanagatari. Born in 1981 in Japan, he grew up deeply influenced by manga culture and originally aspired to become a mangaka before deciding that his strengths lay in writing prose rather than drawing. He briefly attended Ritsumeikan University’s College of Policy Science but left without graduating, soon turning his full attention to fiction.

In 2002 Nisio debuted with “Kubikiri Cycle,” the first volume of the Zaregoto series, winning the prestigious 23rd Mephisto Prize at around twenty years of age and establishing himself as one of the youngest notable mystery and light novel writers of his generation. Over the following years he produced a remarkable body of work at high speed, writing across mystery, fantasy, and metafiction, and collaborating with major publishers such as Kodansha on magazines like Faust and Pandora. Many of his works have been adapted into anime and other media, with the Monogatari series—encompassing titles like “Kizumonogatari” and later arcs such as “Owarimonogatari” and “Koyomimonogatari”—becoming a landmark franchise in 21st‑century Japanese pop culture.

Nisio’s fiction is characterized by fast, witty, and wordplay‑heavy dialogue, dense intertextual references to manga and anime, and psychologically eccentric characters often engaged in philosophical or comedic banter. Between 2009 and 2016 he consistently ranked among Japan’s best‑selling authors, at times topping national sales charts, and his novels and manga have sold tens of millions of copies. Despite using a pseudonym and keeping many personal details private, he has become a defining figure in modern Japanese genre literature, influencing both light novels and mainstream fiction while maintaining close relationships with illustrators, editors, and fellow creators across the manga and anime industries.
