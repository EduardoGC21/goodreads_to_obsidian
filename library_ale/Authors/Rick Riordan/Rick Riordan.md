## Biography
Rick Riordan (1964– ) is an American author best known for creating the Percy Jackson & the Olympians series, which blends modern adventure with classical Greek mythology. Raised in Texas, he studied English and history and worked for many years as a middle-school teacher, an experience that informed his approachable, humorous writing voice and his sensitivity to how young readers connect with stories. The character of Percy Jackson began as a bedtime story for his son, who was interested in Greek myths and living with ADHD and dyslexia—traits Riordan wove into Percy’s character to offer a heroic reflection for children with similar experiences.

With the publication of "The Lightning Thief" and its sequels, including "The Sea of Monsters," "The Titan’s Curse," "The Battle of the Labyrinth," and "The Last Olympian," Riordan became a defining figure in contemporary middle-grade fantasy. His work is regarded for reintroducing classical mythology to a new generation, using fast-paced plots and relatable, diverse characters to make ancient stories feel immediate and relevant. Beyond Percy Jackson, he expanded his mythological universes to include Egyptian, Roman, and Norse traditions, and has also championed other writers through Rick Riordan Presents, an imprint that supports diverse myth-inspired fiction, further cementing his influence on children’s literature and representation in publishing.
