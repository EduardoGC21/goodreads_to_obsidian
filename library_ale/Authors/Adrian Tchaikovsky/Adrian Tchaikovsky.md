## Biography
Adrian Tchaikovsky, born Adrian Czajkowski in Woodhall Spa, Lincolnshire in 1972, is a British writer of fantasy and science fiction whose work is strongly shaped by his fascination with the natural world and non-human minds. He studied zoology and psychology at the University of Reading before qualifying as a legal executive, working in law in Reading and Leeds for many years while writing fiction in his spare time. After a long path to publication, he emerged as a major voice in speculative fiction with series such as *Shadows of the Apt* and the later *Children of Time* books, which explore themes of evolution, intelligence, and the alien.

Over the course of his career, Tchaikovsky has become one of the most prolific and decorated British genre authors of his generation. His novel *Children of Time* won the Arthur C. Clarke Award in 2016, and subsequent works have received the British Fantasy, British Science Fiction, BSFA, and Sidewise Awards, among others. Beyond his larger series, he has written numerous standalone novels, novellas, and short fiction that range from epic fantasy to hard science fiction and horror, often returning to questions about culture, consciousness, and the ethics of power and technology.

Tchaikovsky lives in Leeds with his wife and son. Outside writing, he is known as a keen role-player and board gamer, with training in stage-fighting and a long-standing interest in entomology and historical combat. His background in zoology and psychology underpins his interest in non-human perspectives, helping to make works such as *Children of Time* and later novels like *Shroud* distinctive for their richly imagined societies and the complex relationships they depict between humans, animals, and artificial beings.
