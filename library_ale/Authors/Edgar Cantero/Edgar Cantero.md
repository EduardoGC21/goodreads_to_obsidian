## Biography
Edgar Cantero (born 1981 in Barcelona) is a Spanish writer and cartoonist whose career has unfolded across Catalan, Spanish, and English. Trained in the humanities, he first gained attention in Catalonia in his twenties through prize‑winning short stories and novellas before breaking out with his debut novel “Dormir amb Winona Ryder,” which won major Catalan literary awards and marked him as an inventive new voice in postmodern narrative. Alongside prose, he built a parallel career as a scriptwriter and comics artist for the satirical magazine *El Jueves*, sharpening the irreverent humor and visual sense that would later infuse his fiction.

From this local success, Cantero moved onto the international stage, publishing the gothic mystery “The Supernatural Enhancements” (also known in Spanish as “El factor sobrenatural”), the horror‑comedy bestseller “Meddling Kids,” and the genre‑bending noir “This Body’s Not Big Enough for Both of Us” with major Anglophone publishers. His work, noted for playful experimentation with form, pop‑culture allusions, and a blend of horror, mystery, and dark comedy, has earned both critical acclaim and a popular readership, particularly in the United States and the United Kingdom. Despite his international profile, he has periodically returned to writing in Catalan, maintaining ties to the literary culture in which he started.

Cantero’s fiction often foregrounds strong, eccentric characters and subversive twists on familiar genres, reflecting his background in comics and satire as well as his interest in genre history. Having lived both in Spain and in the United States, he occupies a hybrid cultural space, moving comfortably between European and American traditions while preserving a distinctively idiosyncratic voice. His ongoing output across languages, formats, and media has made him a notable contemporary figure in horror and speculative fiction.
