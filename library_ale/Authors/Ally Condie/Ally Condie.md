## Biography
Ally Condie (born 1978) is an American author best known for her young adult and middle-grade fiction, particularly the dystopian "Matched" trilogy. Raised in Cedar City, Utah, she studied English teaching at Brigham Young University and worked as a high school English teacher before turning to writing full time. Her experience in the classroom and her engagement with teens deeply shaped her interest in stories about identity, choice, and resistance within tightly controlled societies.

Condie’s novels often explore themes of individuality, family bonds, and moral responsibility in speculative settings. With works like "Matched" and "Atlantia," she became a significant voice in early-2010s young adult literature, contributing to the wave of dystopian and imaginative fiction that resonated with both teen and adult readers. She has continued to publish for younger audiences as well, building a body of work that emphasizes emotional depth, ethical questions, and the power of personal agency.

Living in Utah with her family, Condie balances writing with community involvement and literacy advocacy. Her career reflects a sustained commitment to crafting accessible, thought-provoking stories that invite young readers to examine their own choices and the structures that shape their lives.
