## Biography
Adam Fawer (born 1970 in New York City) is an American novelist whose work blends high-concept scientific ideas with fast-paced thriller plots. Before turning to fiction, he built a successful corporate career after studying economics, statistics, and business at the University of Pennsylvania, the Wharton School, and Stanford Graduate School of Business, eventually serving as chief operating officer at About.com and working at companies such as Sony Music and J.P. Morgan. A serious illness in the life of a close friend prompted him to reconsider his priorities and pursue his long-held ambition to write.

Fawer’s debut novel, "Improbable" (2005), a science‑driven thriller about a gifted but troubled gambler whose life is transformed by an experimental treatment, became an international success, was translated into many languages, and won the 2006 International Thriller Writers Award for Best First Novel. He went on to publish further novels, including "Gnosis" (also known as "Empathy") and "Oz," extending his interest in probability, consciousness, and the limits of human knowledge into different narrative settings. Although he has kept a relatively private public profile, Fawer is often noted for the way his mathematical and business background informs the intricate structures and speculative premises of his fiction.

He has spent much of his life in New York City, where he lives with his partner and children. His work is frequently cited as an example of commercially successful fiction that remains intellectually ambitious, and "Improbable" in particular continues to be his most widely recognized contribution to contemporary popular thrillers.
